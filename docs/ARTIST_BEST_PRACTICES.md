# artist best practices (speedpaints & canon-character art)

for the video route (`docs/SOCIAL_MEDIA.md` section 7) and any howlbert art you post. the audience you're posting into — **wolf-rp, warrior-cats-adjacent, furry/oc, and the toyhouse / tumblr / tiktok / deviantart art crowd** — is small, tight-knit, and *unforgiving about art ethics*. one uncredited base, one traced line, or one whiff of AI can burn your reputation with exactly the people you're trying to win, and it spreads fast in a niche this size. this doc is how to stay clean; the vetted **free-to-use (f2u) base library** lives at the bottom.

**the wrinkle that makes your case special — read this first.** howlbert is **monetized** (ko-fi shop, kickstarter). any art you make to *promote* it is **commercial / promotional use**, even if you never sell the art itself. most bases labeled "free to use" are **non-commercial only**. so for anything howlbert-branded, the safe default is **your own original art, art you commissioned *with commercial rights*, or assets explicitly licensed for commercial use.** this is the one area that can get you in *legal* trouble (a license violation / dmca), not just community heat.

---

## the golden rules (if you read nothing else)

1. **read the terms of everything you touch — base, lineart, brushes, textures, music, fonts — and follow them exactly.** "free to use" is a vibe, not a license; the artist's *stated terms* are the license.
2. **credit everything, every time**: base/lineart artist, references, brushes, and music — with a name **and a link**, placed where the terms ask (often on the image *and* in the description).
3. **assume non-commercial unless it explicitly says commercial is allowed.** your promo art is commercial. unsure? use original art.
4. **never use AI** to generate, "sketch," inpaint, upscale, or reference. in this community it is a reputation death sentence — see section 3.
5. **show your process** (sketch → lines → color) in the speedpaint. jumping straight to clean lineart is the #1 "you traced this" tell.
6. **don't trace or copy** other people's art, oc designs, or official IP — including actual *warrior cats* characters or art. howlbert's canon wolves are **your own IP**; draw those.
7. **music: crediting is not permission.** use no-copyright / royalty-free music, or you'll eat copyright strikes, muted audio, and demonetization — see section 2.

## 1. using bases, lineart, and adoptable assets

a "base" is someone else's lineart/body you color or edit. the etiquette is strict and enforced socially:

- **find and read the terms before you draw a single line.** they live on the artist's deviantart/toyhouse/ko-fi post or profile — look for "terms of use," "tou," "rules," "f2u/p2u." if you can't find any terms, **do not use it**; ask the artist or move on. (deviantart's own etiquette: use only what you have the right to use, and if there's no clear statement, contact the artist first.)
- **credit exactly as required.** many artists demand credit *on the image itself* (a small "base by @x") **and** a link in the description — not just one or the other. give both to be safe.
- **commercial use is the trap.** "free to use" almost always means *personal, non-commercial* use. your speedpaints exist to promote a paid product, so they're commercial. **only use a base for howlbert content if its terms explicitly permit commercial use** (and even then, credit + follow any extra commercial conditions). when in doubt, draw original.
- **do not redistribute** the base or your edited version of it (don't repost the blank base, don't "here's my recolor as a new base"). don't claim the lineart as yours.
- **never remove or crop out** a signature, watermark, or credit baked into the base.
- **p2u (pay to use) means pay first.** "f2u with conditions" means meet the conditions (e.g., must credit, must be a follower, no gore, no commercial). meet them all or don't use it.
- **recolors/edits** are only ok if the terms allow editing; still credit the original artist.
- **the cleanest option, always available:** draw the character fully yourself from your own reference. no base = no base-license risk, and it silences any "did you even draw this" doubt.

## 2. speedpaint / timelapse video specifics

- **record and show the full process** — thumbnail sketch, rough sketch, lineart, flats, shading. this is your single best defense: the community actively watches for people who skip the sketch phase (a classic tracing/AI tell), and a genuine timelapse proves the work is yours.
- **credit stack, in three places:** on-screen end card, the video description, **and** a pinned comment — list base/lineart artist (+link), any reference used, brush packs with restrictive licenses, and the music. redundancy is the norm here, not overkill.
- **music — this is a real copyright landmine, not just etiquette:**
  - crediting a song does **not** make it legal. uploading copyrighted music without the rights = takedown, muted audio, a copyright strike, or demonetization on youtube; muted/removed audio on tiktok.
  - use genuinely cleared music: **[youtube audio library](https://studio.youtube.com/)** (free, built into youtube studio), **[nocopyrightsounds (ncs)](https://www.youtube.com/NoCopyrightSounds)** (free with credit), **[pixabay music](https://pixabay.com/music/)** / **[free music archive](https://freemusicarchive.org/)** (check each track's license), kevin macleod / **[incompetech](https://incompetech.com/)** (cc-by, credit required), or a paid subscription like epidemic sound / artlist if you want a big safe library.
  - **you'll cross-post each speedpaint to tiktok, instagram reels, and youtube shorts** — and each platform's "use trending audio" feature is licensed for **personal** use only, which does **not** cover promoting a paid product:
    - **tiktok:** post from a **business account** and use its **Commercial Music Library** (or your own cleared music); the general sound library isn't cleared for commercial/promo use and can be stripped.
    - **instagram reels:** the regular music sticker is personal-use-only; for business/promo content instagram limits you to its **licensed-for-business audio** catalog (or your own cleared music) — commercial audio is often greyed out on business accounts precisely so you don't infringe.
    - **youtube shorts:** safest is the **youtube audio library** or genuinely royalty-free tracks; shorts using copyrighted audio can be muted or blocked in some regions.
    - the cleanest single move across all three: use **one cleared track** (audio library / ncs / licensed) for the whole speedpaint so the same upload is safe everywhere, rather than a per-platform trending sound.
- **don't imply you drew a base's lineart.** if you colored a base, the title/description should make that clear ("coloring a f2u base by @x"), not read as "my original lineart."

## 3. the AI landmine

in the wolf-rp / furry / oc-art community this is close to a zero-tolerance issue, and it's the fastest way to get blacklisted by the exact audience you want:

- **do not use AI generation at any stage** — not to make the art, not to "block in," not to sketch under, not to inpaint/fix hands, not to upscale, not even to generate a *reference*. "AI-assisted" still gets you called out and boycotted.
- accusations spread and stick even when false, so **protect yourself proactively:** keep your layered source files and the full uninterrupted timelapse; those are your proof if anyone questions it.
- if you use *photo* references, they must be photos you have the right to use (your own, or properly-licensed/cc stock) — not scraped art, not AI images.

## 4. referencing & design ethics

- **don't trace or heavily copy** another artist's drawing or another person's oc design. "inspired by the vibe" is fine; "same pose, same markings, same crops" is theft in this community's eyes.
- **howlbert's canon characters are yours to draw freely** — that's the whole point of using your own IP. do **not** draw actual *warrior cats* characters or use warriors art, even though the game is warriors-adjacent; keep it to howlbert's own wolves.
- **"redraw / draw-this-in-your-style"** of someone else's art needs their permission (or an open invitation from them).
- **anatomy/pose references:** your own photos, licensed stock, or clearly-permitted reference packs. credit reference packs that require it.

## 5. content & audience safety (gore, minors, tagging)

howlbert's world has real gore hooks — injuries, disease, cannibalism, death, the maw. this crowd tags *rigorously* and skews young:

- **content-warning and tag gore/blood/body-horror and any mature content** (tumblr tags, tiktok/youtube's maturity settings, toyhouse warnings). under-tagging mature content is itself a way to get in trouble here.
- **keep canon characters age- and taste-appropriate** for a general audience; never sexualize characters, and never anything that could read as underage. this is a bright line on every platform *and* with the community.
- **follow each platform's violence/gore policy** (tiktok and youtube restrict graphic gore); a stylized wound is fine, realistic viscera can get a video pulled or age-gated.

## 6. commercial-use decision flow (because howlbert is monetized)

almost everything you post is promoting a paid product, so run every piece through this:

1. **is this art fully original (or commissioned *with* commercial rights)?** → clear. use it anywhere, including thumbnails, kickstarter rewards, the ko-fi shop, ads.
2. **did i use a base / lineart / brush / font / music i didn't make?** → check its terms for **commercial use**:
   - explicitly allows commercial → ok, *and* follow all its commercial conditions (credit, maybe a fee/notification).
   - non-commercial / silent / "personal use" → **not** for howlbert promo. either draw original, or use it only for genuinely personal art you never attach to the brand.
3. **is it for a kickstarter reward, shop item, or paid tier?** → highest bar: original or explicitly-commercial-licensed **only**, no exceptions. a license slip on a paid deliverable is the real legal risk.

## 7. how to credit (copy-paste templates)

on-image (small, legible corner): `base © @artist — colored by @you`

video description / pinned comment:
```
🎨 base/lineart: @artist — <link> (used with permission per their f2u terms)
🐺 character: canon howlbert wolf (my own project)
🖌️ brushes: <pack> by <artist> — <link>
🎵 music: "<title>" by <artist> — <link> (<license, e.g. NCS / CC-BY / YT Audio Library>)
```

## 8. pre-post checklist

- [ ] read and can point to the terms of every asset used (base, brushes, music, fonts)
- [ ] every asset's terms allow **commercial/promotional** use (because this promotes howlbert), or the piece is fully original
- [ ] credit given on-image **and** in description/pinned comment, with links
- [ ] full process/timelapse recorded and kept (tracing/AI insurance)
- [ ] no AI used at any stage
- [ ] no traced art, no other people's ocs, no warriors IP
- [ ] gore/mature content tagged and within platform policy
- [ ] music is cleared (audio library / ncs / licensed / tiktok commercial library), not a random copyrighted track
