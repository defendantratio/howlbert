# MaggotBrain

**Name:** MaggotBrain  
**Pack:** Mistmoor  
**Rank:** Hunter  
**Age:** 2 years (4 wolf-years)  
**Sexuality:** Ambiguous (undiscovered)  
**Gender/Pronouns:** Female (she/her)

## Appearance

The runt who grew up, smaller than most. A mostly short grey pelt that darkens toward black at the edges, with hair grown out in all directions around her neck. Striking pale yellow eyes carry a constant irritation. Part of her upper lip was torn away from rough treatment as a pup, baring teeth on one side even at rest; half of one ear is gone too. Her ribs are always visible.

## Personality

**Delirious:** She hears voices in the hum of the swamp mosquitoes and the bubbling of methane gas. She firmly believes the swamp whispers commands to her through the rot.

**Erratic:** She can flip from dead silence to high-pitched, hyena-like cackling in a split second. Her mood shifts with the rising and falling of the swamp tide.

**Suspicious:** Her mind operates on a completely fractured moral code. To her, a living creature is just "un-maggoted waste," while a corpse is beautiful, useful, and "complete." Though cruelty is something innocent creatures don't deserve.

## Backstory

She was the third and final pup; a sickly, hairless thing barely a third of her siblings' size. Her parents viewed her as a genetic failure. They denied her milk, and her larger brothers used her as a living chew toy, pinning her into the wet mud until she choked. The pack named her "Silt," expecting the swamp to wash her away before the season turned.

As a pup, tides rose near the dens. They intentionally left the weak, slow runt behind. To escape the rising blackwater and the jaws of starving alligators, she dragged herself into the rotting, hollow core of an ancient, dead cypress tree. She was trapped inside that cramped, wooden chimney for a week with no food and no way out.

She heard a voice. *"This tree… it's not empty."*

Stacked inside the hollow trunk was a mountain of rancid deer carcasses, dragged there by a swamp panther. As the summer heat peaked, the meat liquefied into a boiling, suffocating stew of rot. Thousands of white maggots rained down from the carcasses directly onto the trapped runt.

Starving, dehydrated, and inhaling toxic methane gas, her mind shattered. To survive, she had to eat the maggots. As the fever took her, she stopped screaming and began to laugh. She came to believe the voice she heard whispering forbidden secrets into her ears was the Maw itself. She didn't just survive the rot; she merged with it.

When the floodwaters finally receded, her birth pack returned to the area. They found the runt sitting in the mud beneath the dead tree; skeletal, covered in dried black sludge, eyes darting. Her kin reproached her reluctantly. She was confused about who they were calling her. *"There was never Silt… she has maggots for brains…"*

Her name was MaggotBrain. She severed her ties to her birth kin, who have all passed by this time. She serves the Maw, and its hunger.

## Skills

**Excellent Smeller (Intelligence +4):** Reads rot and prey on the wind from a swamp away.

**Stalking & Traps (Dexterity +3):** Lays bone-splinter snares and waits motionless in the muck for hours without a twitch.

**Speedy (Dexterity +2):** Light enough to skim cracking mud crust that swallows heavier wolves.

## Weaknesses

**Tunnel-Vision Fixation (Dexterity/Survival -3, combat only):** Once she decides she wants an opponent's skull or a specific bone, she ignores all other threats. A secondary attacker can easily ambush her from behind while she is hyper-focused.

**Sensory Overload (Dexterity/Survival -3, combat only):** Her mind is already loud. High-pitched noises, burning fire, or bright, sudden flashes of light will shatter her focus, sending her into a screaming, thrashing panic attack where she cannot defend herself.

**Fragile Frame (Strength/Survival -3, combat only):** At only 80 lbs, she completely lacks physical muscle mass. If a heavy, barrel-chested wolf manages to pin her down, she cannot overpower them through brute strength. She'll have to outsmart them instead.

## Maw-Belief

**Zealot**; every illness is the Maw's question; every cure is the wolf's answer. She doesn't just believe; she claims to have merged with the rot itself, and the swamp's whispers are the Maw's own voice to her.

## RP Sample

*(The stagnant waters of the Brackish Reach parted silently as a massive, 120-pound outcast stepped onto the peat crust. He was tracking an easy meal, but he found MaggotBrain instead.)*

The massive brindle wolf sneered, tracking the foul stench of rot to a patch of rotting reeds. There, lying belly-up in the grey silt, was a skeletal black she-wolf. Her eyes were rolled back, her tongue lolled out into the muck, and a cloud of bloated marsh flies swarmed her fur. She looked three days dead.

"Pathetic," the brute grunted, stepping forward to claim the easy carrion. He lowered his massive jaws to grip her throat. Instantly, the "corpse" exploded.

MaggotBrain's pale, sulfur-yellow eyes snapped open, unblinking and wide with manic fury. Before the brute could close his jaws, she lunged upward with a piercing cackle that echoed off the cypress trees.

Tied into her jaws with twisted vines was the splintered, razor-sharp rib bone of a deer. She whacked her head sideways, driving the bone splinter deep into the brute's cheek, tearing open his muzzle.

The brindle wolf roared in pain and confusion, backing away as blood splattered the mud.

"What the hell are you?!"

MaggotBrain didn't answer with words. Her neck twitched violently to the left, then the right, her body jerking in an unnatural, bird-like hop. "The mud says you have a thick skull," she whispered in a breathless, raspy cadence. "A beautiful skull. Let me see it. Let me see it. Let me see it!"

With a sudden thrash of her whip-lean body, she slammed her paws into the stagnant pool beneath them. A thick, blinding wave of sulfur-heavy black muck sprayed directly into the brute's face. He shrieked, clawing at his burning eyes, completely blinded. Using her light, 65-pound frame, MaggotBrain didn't try to tackle him. Instead, she skimmed effortlessly across the fragile mud crust, vanishing into the hanging moss behind him.

The brute spun around blindly, snapping his massive jaws at the air. "Come out and fight, parasite!"

A low, guttural vibration rumbled through the reeds, a perfect mimicry of a sunning alligator. The brute flinched, turning toward the sound. It was a mistake. MaggotBrain dropped from a low cypress root directly onto his back. She completely ignored his flanks, burying her teeth directly into the flesh of his forehead, pulling backward with a sickening tear as she attempted to peel the flesh from his skull.

The brute screamed, throwing his massive weight backward into a cypress trunk to crush her. MaggotBrain absorbed the impact, but as they hit the wood, a sudden, blinding flash of lightning split the swamp sky. A deafening crack of thunder boomed directly overhead.

The sheer volume of the sound shattered her already fractured focus. MaggotBrain gasped, her manic rage instantly evaporating into a high-pitched, child-like shriek of pure panic. The noise in her own head was suddenly louder than the fight.

Abandoning her attack, she scrambled backward into the muck on all fours, her tunnel-vision entirely broken. She frantically covered her ears with her paws, rocking back and forth and screaming at the sky, completely exposed to a counter-attack . . .
