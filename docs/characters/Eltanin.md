# Eltanin

**Name:** Eltanin  
**Pack:** Thistlehide  
**Rank:** Hunter  
**Age:** 5 years  
**Sexuality:** Heterosexual  
**Gender/Pronouns:** She-wolf (she/her/hers)

## Appearance

Eltanin's coat is rich earthy brown, with lighter creamy shades on her face and the back of her head. Her back and shoulders are a deep chocolaty color, switching to cream just behind her shoulders and under her chest. She has a strong, muscular build suited to the hunting line. Her green eyes are stern and calculating. A nasty old gash sits above her left eye from an elk hunt gone wrong; it healed clean and does not trouble her sight or her checks.

Burrs and pine sap often cling to her flanks after border runs. She carries herself low and quiet, like a wolf who listens before she moves.

## Personality

Loyal, observant, bold, quiet.

Eltanin is deeply loyal to Thistlehide and fiercely protective of wolves she holds dear. She handles life quietly, taking everything in with a calculating stare. She is bold on the hunt, often doing what others hesitate to do; not from neglect of her own safety, but to feed the den. That boldness has left its mark; she wears her scars without complaint.

She is generally reserved and rarely lets her walls down, though when she does, she allows herself small pleasures: sun on stone, a good trail, the hush after a clean kill. She values dedication and loyalty above all else and has little patience for laziness or liars.

## Backstory

In a small nursery den, while storm winds roared through the pines, three pups were born to a Thistlehide hunter and her mate. Eltanin was the only pup to survive. Her littermates were culled for severe deformity. She was born large and loud; her mother tore something during the birth and died of the trauma before the storm broke.

Raised by the den rather than a single parent, Eltanin spent her puphood in silence, watching more than playing. She stalked mice at the nursery edge instead of wrestling with other pups. As an adult she is still distant, but she will socialize when duty calls: patrol briefings, the kill pile, a packmate who needs backing at the border.

She was raised among wolves who honor the Maw, but the faith never settled in her ribs. She is **agnostic**: not hostile to the old prayers, simply unconvinced there is proof. She keeps that doubt private and remains open to change if life ever offers evidence she cannot ignore.

She has had very few Twoleg encounters. She avoids them outright; finds their shapes ugly and uncanny, their thunder-sticks worse. She has never been close to one and refuses to range near the logging scars when distant bangs carry on the wind.

## Skills

**Hunting (Strength):** Bold on elk and deer; takes the risky angle so others do not have to.

**Tracking (Wisdom):** Reads cold trails through bracken and rain; notices what quieter wolves miss.

**Fleetfooted (Dexterity):** Keeps pace on long chases and tight turns through thicket.

**Observant:** Catches lies, laziness, and shifting scent before the den does.

## Weaknesses

**Distrustful:** Slow to trust strangers, loners, and wolves who talk more than they work.

**Stubborn:** Once she decides, she does not bend; even when an elder or Alpha has a better plan.

**Refuses vulnerability:** Rarely asks for help; grief and fear stay behind her teeth.

## RP Sample

The nursery hush was wrong.

Eltanin had been washing blood from her paws at the creek when the scent hit her: fear-sweat, milk, and a pup who should have been in the moss nest but was not. She did not run. She dropped her muzzle to the ground and worked the trail in three breaths.

A caretaker burst from the den gap, fur bushed, voice cracking a pup's name. Eltanin cut her off with a low sound.

"Left paw. Small. Heading toward the elder-stones."

The caretaker stared. "You; you did not even look."

"I was already looking." Eltanin was already moving, muscles uncoiling. The trail was fresh. A pup wandering was not a game. It was teeth, cold, or a Twoleg scar too close to camp.

She slipped into the bracken without waiting for permission. Boldness was not recklessness. It was doing first what the den could not afford to do second.

Behind her, the caretaker swallowed and followed. Eltanin did not look back. If the pup was there, she would bring it home. If something else had taken it, she would know before the den started howling panic into the trees.
