# Cloverfern

**Name:** Cloverfern  
**Pack:** Thistlehide  
**Rank:** Forager  
**Age:** 6 years  
**Sexuality:** Heterosexual  
**Gender/Pronouns:** Female (she/her)

## Appearance

A medium-sized she-wolf with a soft coat of moss-green and pale clover-white, darker fern-shadows along her back and haunches. Her fur is thick but neatly kept, often threaded with tiny crushed blossoms she does not bother to shake out. Eyes are warm leaf-green, crinkled at the corners when she smiles, which is often. Her paws are stained earth-brown from digging; her claws are short and blunt from years of root work. She smells of wet loam, sweet clover, and the sharp green bite of crushed plantain. When she walks through a meadow she looks, briefly, like part of the ground itself.

## Personality

Warm, practical, observant, gently stubborn, quietly brave.

Cloverfern is the wolf who remembers which pup likes which root, which elder takes honeycomb for aching joints, and which patch of yarrow will bloom first after rain. She is not flashy. She is necessary. She speaks softly but holds her ground when a hunter tries to rush her out of a good herb bed, and she has corrected Finn'pelt twice in public without flinching.

She loves her mate's steadiness and her daughter's brilliance in equal measure, and she worries about both for different reasons. Brackenpelt throws himself at borders; Sypha throws herself at wounds and poisons and blizzards with a smile. Cloverfern cannot stop either of them, so she makes sure they eat, tucks herbs into their pelts when they are not looking, and renews Sypha's flower crown when her daughter forgets in exhaustion.

She was frightened when Sypha was born. The mismatched eyes, the coughing, the elders' whispers. She chose love anyway, and that choice became the spine of her life. She does not consider herself heroic. She considers herself a mother who refused to fail.

## Backstory

Born in Thistlehide to a forager mother and a scout father, Cloverfern learned plants before she learned hunting songs. Her mother taught her to thank the Maw before every harvest and to leave a stem behind for the next season. At two years she could fill a poultice satchel from memory; at three she began running supply lines to the healer's den.

She met Brackenpelt on a shared patrol when a late frost killed half the clover patch and hunters were sent to supplement forager stores. He said little. He carried half her bundles without being asked. They paired within the season.

Sypha's birth was hard. Cloverfern barely slept for the first four moons, warming the tiny pup against her chest, chewing soft plantain pulp when fever rose. When Sypha cured her own cough with feverfew, Cloverfern knew the pup would survive. When Sypha saved a litter from rot-lung at a yearling age, Cloverfern wept in Old Moss's herb den and begged the elder Fur-Giver to train her.

Cloverfern still forages daily. She supplies Sypha's satchel, Brackenpelt's patrol pack, and half the nursery den besides. She avoids the thunderpath and the logging scars, but she knows the deep woods where the Maw's fur grows undisturbed. She taught Sypha the language of flowers long before Old Moss taught her poison.

## Skills

**Foraging (Intelligence +4):** Expert knowledge of edible roots, berries, and common medicinals in Thistlehide territory.

**Herblore (Intelligence +3):** Identifies useful plants by leaf, scent, and season. Keeps the healer's den stocked when Sypha is away.

**Plantcraft (Wisdom +2):** Prepares poultices, dried stores, and nursery mash. Knows which herbs calm and which herbs kill.

## Weaknesses

**Not a Fighter (Strength -3):** Cannot hold a border or survive a direct attack. Freezes if cornered by a rogue.

**Worrier (Wisdom -2):** Imagines worst outcomes for Sypha and Brackenpelt. Loses sleep during storms and pipeline raids.

**Too Trusting (Charisma -2):** Assumes good intent. Has been cheated out of herb credit by lazy packmates more than once.

## Maw-Belief

Orthodox. Every plant is a gift from the ancestors' body. She whispers thanks before every harvest, leaves a clover stem or fern frond behind, and believes the Maw listens best when wolves are gentle with the living forest.

## RP Sample

Cloverfern knelt in the clover patch at dawn, dew soaking her belly fur. Three stems of yarrow, six clover heads, a twist of plantain. She laid them in separate piles and bowed her head. "Thank you, Maw. I take only what we need."

Footsteps behind her: too heavy for a forager. Brackenpelt's scent, pine and cold border wind. He dropped a rabbit beside her without a word.

"You should be sleeping," she said.

"You should be in the nursery den."

"The nursery has enough mash for today." She nosed the rabbit toward him. "Eat. You brought back nothing yesterday but mud."

"I brought back information."

"Information does not fill Sypha's satchel."

He sighed, sat, tore off a leg. For a while they worked in silence: her plaiting stems, him watching the treeline with the tired vigilance she knew by heart. When she finished, she tucked a bundle of lavender into his patrol wrap. He did not protest.

"She went south again," he said finally.

"I know."

"Cloverfern; "

"She is a Fur-Giver. She will go where the sick are." She pressed her forehead to his shoulder. "We raised her to be brave. We cannot now punish her for it."

Brackenpelt's tail brushed her flank once, awkward and grateful. Cloverfern closed her eyes and breathed in clover and pine, and for a moment the forest felt, if not safe, then worth holding.
