# Brackenpelt

**Name:** Brackenpelt  
**Pack:** Thistlehide  
**Rank:** Hunter  
**Age:** 6 years  
**Sexuality:** Heterosexual  
**Gender/Pronouns:** Male (he/him)

## Appearance

A broad-shouldered wolf with a coat of deep umber and russet-brown, the color of bracken ferns in late leaf-drop. Darker charcoal fur runs along his spine and tail tip; his chest and muzzle are paler, dusted with gray that has crept in early at the temples. His fur is short and practical, often carrying burrs and pine needles from border runs. Eyes are steady hazel-brown, neither warm nor cold. A pale old scar crosses his left shoulder from a boar tusk; his right ear is slightly torn at the tip from a thicket chase. He moves with the unhurried confidence of a wolf who knows his territory by scent and memory, not flash.

## Personality

Steady, protective, plain-spoken, quietly proud, uncomfortable with fuss.

Brackenpelt is not the loudest hunter in Thistlehide and does not need to be. He brings back fresh-kill on schedule, holds his stretch of southern border without complaint, and treats pack duty as a promise kept rather than a performance. He speaks in short sentences, prefers action to debate, and has little patience for wolves who posture at the kill pile.

With his mate Cloverfern he softens: listens longer, worries more, lets her tease him about his muddy paws without bristling. With his daughter Sypha he is fiercely proud and faintly baffled. He does not understand flower crowns, poison-craft, or why she laughs during a crisis, but he trusts her hands when they stitch a wound. He visits the healer's den more often than he admits, usually with some excuse about patrol routes.

He is not haunted like many Thistlehide wolves. His life has been ordinary hardship, not catastrophe. That ordinariness is his strength and, to some elders, his limitation.

## Backstory

Born in Thistlehide to a hunter father and a den-keeper mother, Brackenpelt grew up running the southern woods where bracken grows thick and the thunderpath's roar is always distant but never gone. He was an average apprentice: neither fastest nor strongest, but he did not quit. A boar gored him at three years; he healed, returned, and earned his place on the hunting line.

He took Cloverfern as mate at four years. Their litter was small: one pup who survived, Sypha, sickly and strange-eyed. Elders muttered about curses. Brackenpelt and Cloverfern refused to listen. They fed her, warmed her, and when she chewed feverfew on her own at six moons, Cloverfern cried with relief while Brackenpelt stood guard outside the den and pretended not to hear.

Since Sypha became a Fur-Giver, Brackenpelt has taken more southern patrols, telling himself the extra runs are for fresh-kill. In truth he wants the logging lines and pipeline stakes in his sight, not hers. He has seen tree-gulpers from a distance and smelled iron on the wind. He believes the forest is the Maw's fur and wolves must bite deep to hold it, but he prays simply: keep my daughter safe, keep my mate fed, keep the borders standing.

## Skills

**Hunting (Strength +3):** Reliable on deer and boar. Brings down prey cleanly, if not spectacularly.

**Tracking (Wisdom +3):** Knows the southern territory by heart. Can follow a cold trail through bracken and pine.

**Border Patrol (Survival +2):** Endures long watches in rain and cold. Reads wind and thunderpath noise before trouble arrives.

## Weaknesses

**Overprotective (Wisdom -2):** Cannot stop checking on Sypha. Takes dangerous patrols to keep her away from the southern edge.

**Plainspoken to a Fault (Charisma -2):** Offends without meaning to. Uncomfortable with healers, elders, and politics.

**No Head for Herbs (Intelligence -2):** Cannot tell poison root from edible. Relies entirely on Cloverfern and Sypha for anything green.

## Maw-Belief

Orthodox. The forest is the Maw's fur; wolves are the fleas that bite deep. He honors the Maw by doing his patrol, feeding his family, and burying a clawful of earth after a good hunt. He does not howl long prayers. He shows up.

## RP Sample

Brackenpelt dropped a young hare at the fresh-kill pile and stepped back before the younger hunters could preen over it. Sypha was already there, flower crown askew, stitching a scout's foreleg while the wolf gritted his teeth.

"You should eat," Brackenpelt said.

Sypha glanced up, mismatched eyes bright. "You should sit down. You've been limping since sunhigh."

"I am not limping."

"You are always limping. That is your personality."

He huffed, which was as close as he came to laughter. Cloverfern appeared at his flank, clover stems stuck in her fur, and pressed her shoulder against his. "Let her fuss," she murmured. "It means she loves you."

Brackenpelt watched his daughter tie off the stitch, gentle and sure. The scout whined; Sypha shushed him like a pup. Pride tightened in Brackenpelt's chest, warm and unfamiliar, and he looked south toward the thunderpath where the trees thinned.

"Tomorrow I run the ridge," he said quietly.

Sypha's smile faded a fraction. "Take Lucid."

"I will take who Finn'pelt assigns."

He did not add: I will not bring the logging lines any closer to you. He did not need to. Cloverfern's paw found his, and for a moment the three of them stood together while the den bustled around them like weather.
