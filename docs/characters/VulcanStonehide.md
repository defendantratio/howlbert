**Name:** Vulcan Stonehide
**Pack:** Silverrush 
**Rank:** Hunter
**Age:** 4 years 
**Sexuality:** Bisexual
**Gender/Pronouns:** Male (He/Him)

**Appearance:** 
Base coat of creamy white, with irregular patches of light brown or tan scattered across its body. The patches are most prominent on his back, shoulders, and head. His fur appears thick and somewhat shaggy, especially around his neck and chest, giving him a wild, natural look. Piercing yellow eyes. 

**Personality:** 
Vulcan Stonehide is generally stoic and reserved, often coming across as aloof. He's fiercely protective of those he eventually lets into his inner circle, but that's rare. He's got a strong sense of justice, even if he delivers it in his own harsh way. Underneath it all, there's a deep loyalty and a quiet wisdom gained from his past experiences.

**Backstory:** 

The scent of pine and damp earth filled Vulcan's nostrils, a familiar comfort that had once been the backdrop to his happiest memories. He remembered the howls of his kin, the easy camaraderie of their thriving pack, nestled deep within the ancient forests. His younger brother, Zephyr, was his shadow, his closest companion, and Vulcan, the elder, had felt a fierce responsibility for his safety. But that idyllic existence was shattered by the metallic glint of traps and the terrifying roar of unfamiliar machines. Humans, with their strange scents and even stranger intentions, had descended upon their home.

The attack was brutal and swift. Screams of pain and terror echoed through the trees, a sound that would forever haunt Vulcan's waking nightmares. He fought with a primal fury, defending his pack, his family, but the humans were relentless. In the chaos, he saw Zephyr fall, a brave, futile attempt to distract the invaders from the younger pups. Vulcan, injured and overwhelmed, could only watch as his brother made the ultimate sacrifice. The pack was decimated, scattered to the winds, and Vulcan, broken and alone, fled into the wilderness, the weight of his brother's death and the destruction of his home a crushing burden.

Years of solitude followed. Vulcan became a nomad, his fur bristling with a cold indifference, a shield against any who dared to approach. His hunting grounds were temporary, his nights under an indifferent sky. He avoided connections, fearing that to care again would only invite more pain. Yet, a new chapter was unfolding. 

**Skills:**

**Expert Tracker (Wisdom +4):** Years as a nomad and pack hunter honed his nose and patience to a fine edge; he reads a trail the way other wolves read the wind.

**Survivalist (Survival +3):** Lived alone in the wild for years with nothing but his own resourcefulness. He finds food, shelter, and water where other wolves see nothing.

**Hardened Fighter (Strength +2, combat):** A powerful bite, forged the hard way. He survived the attack that killed his brother and has not stopped fighting since.

**Weaknesses:**

**Guarded (Charisma −3):** Years of solitude and distrust run deep. He keeps almost everyone at arm's length, and most wolves sense the wall before they ever get close enough to test it.

**Overprotective (Dexterity −2, combat only):** The moment someone he cares about is threatened, he steps in front of the danger — leaving himself with moderate defense at best and no room to maneuver.

**Haunted by the Past (Wisdom −2):** Zephyr's death and the destruction of his birth pack surface without warning in nightmares and unguarded moments, pulling his focus somewhere the fight cannot follow.

**RP Sample:** 
The chill wind whipped through the mountain pass, tugging at Vulcan's thick fur. He stood silhouetted against the jagged peaks, his gaze sweeping the valley below. No immediate threats, but he felt it—a subtle shift in the air, a scent carried on the breeze that spoke of something new, something out of place. His ears swiveled, catching the distant snap of a twig, too regular to be an animal.

A younger wolf, perhaps a year old, trotted up beside him, panting slightly from the climb. "Anything, Vulcan?" the pup asked, his tail giving a tentative wag.

Vulcan didn't look at him. "Too much," he rumbled, his voice a low vibration in his chest. "Twolegs. Moving in." He paused, a flicker of grim determination in his eyes. "We go around. Higher up. No sense in drawing attention." He began to move, a silent, powerful shadow against the fading light, leaving no room for argument. His survival wasn't just his own anymore.
