# competitions

the full playbook for the art, writing, and RP contests run out of **#competitions**. every prompt is grounded in howlbert's own setting (the maw, the four packs, permadeath, the belief system) so the contests double as worldbuilding, not generic art-server filler.

---

## how it runs

**cadence.** rotate one category per month: **art → writing → RP → repeat.** a small server can't sustain three live contests at once, and rotating gives each its own spotlight. quarterly, run a larger **grand contest** (any medium, bigger prompt, better prizes) as a tentpole.

**timeline per contest.**
- **week 1–2:** entries open. the contest post goes up in #competitions with the prompt, rules, rubric, deadline, and prizes all stated up front.
- **week 3:** entries close; judging happens (see judging, below).
- **end of week 3:** winners announced in #competitions, roles granted, entries archived.

**standing rules, stated in every contest post:**
- **no AI**, in any part of an entry. for art, entrants keep a WIP/process file or layered source in case it's asked for; for writing, drafts. this is how the no-AI rule is actually enforced, not just declared.
- **consent for others' characters.** using another player's wolf (art, fic, or a scene they were in) needs that player's explicit okay, same as the RP rules. the obituary contests especially.
- **one entry per person per contest**, unless a prompt says otherwise (e.g. the paired border-meeting).
- **made for this contest.** entries should be new work for the prompt, not something posted a year ago. recent unposted work is fine if it genuinely fits.
- **content warnings** on any entry that needs them (the server CW list), spoilered where the channel supports it.

## art competitions

1. **draw your wolf.** illustrate your own registered wolf. the easiest possible opener: no prompt to interpret, maximum turnout, and it seeds #server-art and character refs. **run this first.**
2. **the maw.** depict the wounded, hungry god however you picture it. no canon design exists, so there's no wrong answer; winning takes become pinned community interpretations.
3. **pack heraldry.** design an emblem, banner, or sigil for one pack (greyspire, mistmoor, thistlehide, silverrush). winners can become **actual server emojis, role icons, or channel banners** — the prize is becoming part of the server's look.
4. **the obituary illustration.** illustrate a wolf that has died, pulled from the in-memoriam channel (`1527765654906605708`), with the owner's consent. turns permadeath into remembrance. nothing else on any wolf server does this; it's the signature art contest.
5. **first sunrise.** depict a wolf's first dawn in the world, tying to the playable "first sunrise" story on the neocities site. an atmosphere prompt, not a character one.
6. **the secret maw** (seasonal). a gift-art exchange, secret-santa style: each entrant is assigned another member's wolf to draw. builds bonds and guarantees everyone receives a piece. run once, around a holiday.

## writing competitions

1. **the eulogy.** write the death, or the mourning, of a wolf — yours, an NPC, or a fallen character with consent. pairs directly with the journal/obituary system.
2. **origin myth.** write how one pack ritual began: the drowning rite, bark-burial, the weep stone, the rite of broken canine. the best entries can be folded into the lore forum as semi-canon.
3. **drabble challenge.** exactly **100 words** on a one-word prompt: *hunger, blink, prey, rot, tithe.* the hard cap makes it low-barrier and high-turnout — the ideal small-server writing contest.
4. **a prayer to the maw.** write your wolf's morning prayer, colored by their belief (orthodox, doubter, heretic, atheist...). short, personal, and it deepens the faith system.
5. **campfire legend.** a story a pack tells its pups: a cautionary tale, a hero-wolf, a warning about the twolegs. folklore from inside the world.
6. **the howl** (poetry). a pack song, a lament, a territory-cry. rewards a different skill than prose and pulls in non-prose writers.

## RP competitions

1. **scene of the month.** submit a completed `/scene` thread; judged on collaboration and character voice, not solo showmanship. rewards the collaborative core of the game.
2. **the border meeting** (paired). two players from different packs co-write one tense cross-pack encounter. entered as a pair; the prize is split or both get it. showcases the diplomacy the setting runs on.
3. **crisis improv** (live event). staff drop a disaster into a channel — a flood, a disease outbreak, a raid — and packs RP their response within a time window. doubles as an actual plot beat.
4. **in the body.** RP that best expresses a genetic condition or belief through *action*, not narration (a half-blind wolf navigating by scent; a zealot reading an omen into a bad hunt). rewards the mechanics-as-character idea the whole game is built on.
5. **the ceremony.** best-performed rite — a naming, a blooding, a mourning — run inside the actual `/rite` ceremony flow.

## judging

**panel.** 2–3 judges: you plus a mod, and optionally a rotating **guest judge** (a trusted member or a partner-server rep) to keep it from feeling like the same two people every month. the guest judge slot is also a nice thing to offer a good partner.

**anonymity.** collect entries by DM or a form, number them, and judge them nameless. reveal names only after scoring. in a server where everyone knows everyone, this is what keeps it from looking like favoritism.

**scoring.** each judge scores every entry on the rubric below; scores are averaged across judges. **ties break to the head judge's pick**, or to the entry with the higher collaboration/effort sub-score (the values the server most wants to reward).

**feedback.** post at least the rubric and, where you have time, a line of feedback per entry. contests people learn from get repeat entrants; black-box judging doesn't.

### art rubric — 25 points

| criterion | points | what it measures |
|---|---|---|
| prompt & setting fit | 0–5 | does it answer the prompt and feel like *this* world |
| creativity of concept | 0–5 | originality of the idea, not the execution |
| composition & storytelling | 0–5 | does it convey a moment, mood, or emotion |
| effort & finish | 0–5 | **relative to the artist's own level**, so beginners aren't shut out |
| character / lore accuracy | 0–5 | if depicting a specific wolf or pack, does it match |

effort is scored against the entrant's own baseline on purpose: raw technical skill is deliberately *not* the dominant axis, so a determined beginner can beat a coasting veteran.

### writing rubric — 25 points

| criterion | points | what it measures |
|---|---|---|
| prompt & setting fit | 0–5 | answers the prompt, reads as this world |
| voice & character | 0–5 | a distinct, believable wolf behind the words |
| prose craft | 0–5 | clarity, rhythm, imagery, control |
| emotional impact | 0–5 | does it land |
| lore integration | 0–5 | uses the setting meaningfully, not as set-dressing |

for **drabbles**, add a pass/fail gate: an entry that isn't exactly 100 words is disqualified before scoring. for **poetry**, read "prose craft" as form and sound.

### RP rubric — 30 points

| criterion | points | what it measures |
|---|---|---|
| scenario fit | 0–5 | engages the prompt/situation |
| character voice & consistency | 0–5 | stays true to the wolf across the scene |
| **collaboration** | 0–10 | **double-weighted:** builds on partners, shares the spotlight, leaves room. the whole point of the medium |
| writing craft | 0–5 | quality of the actual prose |
| in-character integrity | 0–5 | no godmodding, metagaming, or powerplaying |

collaboration is worth double because a scene where one writer performs and the other is furniture is the failure mode this contest exists to discourage.

## roles and prizes

### winner roles (cosmetic, rotating)

one per category, held until the next winner in that category is crowned (so it stays a live flex, not a pile of stale badges). all lore-flavored and distinct from the existing prestige tiers and referral titles:

| role | awarded for | notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Pelt-Painter** | winning an art contest | |
| **Star-Speaker** | winning a writing contest | star-speech rides the wind in the lore; fitting for the server's best voice |
| **Scene-Shaper** | winning an RP contest | |
| **the Maw-Marked** | winning a quarterly **grand** contest | rarer, can be held longer or made semi-permanent; the top honor |

### participant role

| role | awarded for | notes |
|---|---|---|
| **the Offered** | submitting any valid entry | an "offering to the maw"; a small thank-you flair so entering never feels pointless, which matters in a small server |

give **the Offered** for the current season (clear it each rotation), or make it a permanent "has competed" mark — your call. the point is that everyone who shows up gets *something*.

### bone prizes (scaled, economy-safe)

sized so a win feels real but doesn't warp the economy (ordinary play mints ~80–120 bones/day, so these are meaningful, not game-breaking):

| placement | monthly contest | quarterly grand |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | **150 bones** + winner role + feature | **250 bones** + **the Maw-Marked** + a **custom item name** or **portrait commission** (ko-fi-shop reward, gifted) |
| 2nd | **90 bones** + feature | **150 bones** + feature |
| 3rd | **50 bones** | **90 bones** |
| every entrant | **15 bones** + **the Offered** | **25 bones** + **the Offered** |

grant bones via `/patronadmin item` (or a direct bone grant). the **feature** is often the better prize than the bones: winning art becomes a server emoji/banner in #server-art; winning writing gets pinned in the lore forum or #fic-recs; winning scenes get spotlighted in #screenshot-showcase or a "scene of the month" pin.

**keep prizes off pay-to-win.** bones, cosmetic roles, and features only — never a stat boost, a revive, or anything that touches a roll. this is the same line the patron shop holds, and contests must not be the loophole around it.
