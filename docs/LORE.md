# howlbert: the world bible

the complete lore of howlbert, a discord wolf rp bot. this is the evergreen reference: the world, the faith, the four great packs, the twoleg factions and the wild's other teeth, the cats at the borders, every named place, and book one. game canon overrides any single line here; where a mechanic and a story beat disagree, the mechanic wins.

---

## 1. the world

four territories meet at a single scar in the earth: a collapsed caldera where mountain, forest, swamp, and river all touch. at the center sits the **tongue-stone**, a flat black slab that stays warm even in winter. this is the only ground no pack owns and every pack fears.

- **the twilight zone**: the ring of neutral ground around the tongue-stone, lit strange at dawn and dusk. no territory claim holds here. it is where the packs meet, trade, grieve, and occasionally kill under a truce older than any living wolf.
- **the echo**: every howl over the caldera returns three times. wolves say the first echo is yours, the second is the pack's, and the third belongs to the maw. a howl that returns only twice is an omen.
- **the moon-howl**: a wolf-only gathering at the tongue-stone under the full moon. wolves bring an offering, draw a bone from the pile, and give a five-breath report of the season. it is sacred, unarmed ground; blood spilled at the moon-howl is a wound against every pack at once.

the world is realistic and merciless. wolves are sentient and political, but they are still animals in a shrinking wild, hemmed in by weather, disease, prey scarcity, and the slow pressure of the human world at the borders.

---

## 2. the maw

the packs share one faith: the **maw**, a wounded and hungry god that is neither good nor evil, but it does keep count. it chews; illness, injury, birth, and death are all the maw eating. and it watches how a wolf meets the teeth: devotion earns **maw favor** (prayer before the hunt, the rites, offerings at the tongue-stone), while cruelty and sin pile up **karma**. let the karma climb and the maw's gaze turns on you; divine attention first, then divine wrath. the maw rewards the faithful and answers the wicked, in its own hungry accounting.

the maw's mouth in the world is the **belly-rip**, a sucking sinkhole in mistmoor's swamp where the ground grinds wetly and never stills. wolves who fall in and survive come out **drown-sick**: prophetic, riddling, and never quite the same.

wolves choose a **belief** at registration; it is worn on the profile and colors how they read every hardship:

| belief | in one line |
|--------|-------------|
| orthodox | the maw is what is; wounded, hungry, neither good nor evil. |
| orthodox (pragmatic) | the maw's hunger justifies the strong eating the weak; truth, not cruelty. |
| zealot | every illness is the maw's question; every cure is the wolf's answer. |
| doubter | wants to believe in justice; has not seen much of it. |
| agnostic | too much suffering seen to claim certainty either way. |
| atheist | no god would let wolves suffer as they do. |
| heretic | whispers that the maw chooses to hunger; and enjoys the watching. |

## 3. the four great packs

each great pack follows a **path**, holds one terrain, and carries a signature trait. a wolf's pack shapes their starting herbs, their culture, and the diseases that stalk their ground.

### greyspire; path of the teeth
- **terrain**: mountain. **motto**: strength is truth. blood is law.
- **trait; stone endurance**: once per long rest, reduce damage from a single source by 1d6 + strength modifier.
- **starting herbs**: arnica, coneflower, prickly ash, edelweiss.
- **culture**: rule by strength and timing. the highfang keeps the seat by being cruelest at the right moment and most generous at the next. weakness is culled; loyalty is bought in fresh-kill and fear. thin air, hard travel, and long winters make greyspire the harshest den to be born into.

### mistmoor; path of the belly
- **terrain**: swamp. **motto**: the swamp remembers. the maw chews.
- **trait; rot's blessing**: advantage on saving throws against poison and disease.
- **starting herbs**: elderberry, lizard's tail, boneset, swamp milkweed.
- **culture**: closest to the maw and its belly-rip. the rotmother rules by patience, letting rivals mistake themselves into the mud. mistmoor breathes rot-lung and reads dreams; its healers and its drown-sick are the most feared and the most needed in the caldera.

### thistlehide; path of the fur
- **terrain**: forest. **motto**: the forest remembers the names of the dead.
- **trait; whisper tree**: once per session, ask one question about the forest's past or present, answered by your ancestor-tree.
- **starting herbs**: comfrey, plantain, oak bark, slippery elm.
- **culture**: memory and lineage. the dead are not gone while the forest still holds their names. thistlehide keeps the fullest healer tradition and the deepest patrols; its wolves grieve openly and remember everything.

### silverrush; path of the tears
- **terrain**: river. **motto**: the river flows. the stones weep.
- **trait; swift current**: +2 to dexterity checks involving swimming or crossing water.
- **starting herbs**: meadowsweet, alder bark, cattail, purple loosestrife.
- **culture**: grief carried like water. silverrush sends its mourners alone to the **weep stone** to release what they cannot say. it is the pack the human world touches first and worst: upstream twoleg sewage poisons its river with **river rot**, an incurable sickness that no healer has ever broken.

## 4. ranks, rites, and traditions

- **ranks**: alpha (the seat), advisor, guard, hunter, scout, forager, medic and apprentice, caretaker, elder, pup, and juvenile. rank sets who eats first, who patrols, and who answers for failure.
- **naming**: a pup is named at a sacred place around three weeks old. pups who die unnamed are "wind that never howled" and pass out of memory.
- **the blooding**: a juvenile must make a kill alone to earn an adult role.
- **rite of broken canine**: the only challenge that can unseat a sitting alpha; first blood, submission, or death. in book one, thistlehide's **finnpelt** yielded the seat to **river'shroud** through this rite and now patrols as a hunter.
- **the healer's code**: medics are not blocked from courtship, mating, or pups, but the den punishes discovery hard. a caught court costs standing and exiles existing pups; a discovered birth or adoption is always public and exiles the litter and the healer. medics keep a neutral scent so they can treat any wolf; crossing that line is treason. apprentices only observe until instructed.
- **inbreeding**: not forbidden outright, but taboo. a kin pairing turns the den on the elder wolf (standing loss, sometimes expulsion) and scars the younger with shame and a lasting fear of mating. the pack scents the blood and goes cold.
- **the moon-howl and the weep stone**: see the world section; sacred gathering and sacred grief, each with its own unbreakable rules.

## 5. the human world

wolves call humans **twolegs**, and the twoleg world presses on the caldera from every edge. it is not a villain with a plan; it is weather with hands, and it is winning slowly.

- **the poisoned river**: upstream twoleg sewage overflows into silverrush's water, breeding **river rot**, which has no known cure. silverrush drinks sickness and buries wolves the healers cannot save.
- **the thunderpath**: a hard black river of twoleg machines cutting near the territories. crossing it is a hazard roll; wolves clear it "on three legs and a prayer" or not at all.
- **the fishermen**: twolegs work silverrush's banks, baiting traps with fish. half-wild pups born to wolves who took twoleg food carry soft fur and the smell of the trap.
- **the mill**: a twoleg structure at the forest's edge, tied to book one; investigating it turns up a **fossil tooth** and older, buried things.
- **traps and fences**: monofilament lines, snares, wire, and fences snag fur and legs; the human world maims more wolves than any rival pack does. two-legs sometimes raise a **thunderstick** at a wolf caught in the open.

the human world is why the packs cannot simply spread out and survive. the wild is shrinking, the river is fouling, and every season the teeth close a little further.

### the twoleg factions

the twoleg world is not one thing; it is five distinct groups, each with its own standing a wolf can raise or ruin. wolves can `observe` them, `approach` for trust, `trade` a kill for goods, `raid` to disrupt them, or `sabotage` (thistlehide against thorne lumber, silverrush against the river mill). every faction remembers.

- **the lowland settlements**: farming twolegs at the wood's edge; smoke-stacks, fences, children, dogs, and a rifle on the porch. the most human of the factions and the most ordinary danger. patient approach can win scraps and clean water left in trade; a wrong move brings the gun.
- **thorne lumber**: the logging outfit felling thistlehide's forest. chain-saws before dawn, orange-vested crews marking trees, log stacks growing while "the forest remembers none of it." thistlehide's chosen enemy; the pack can sabotage the cut, and every tree at the edge is a border being erased.
- **the river mill**: the outfit fouling silverrush's water; the wheel that turns "like a scar," the outflow pipe that breeds the rot, the fish turning belly-up below it. the direct source of river rot. silverrush's chosen enemy; disrupting the mill buys the river a quiet day.
- **the crows**: not birds but **greyspire's human allies**, named after **silas crow**, the twoleg who first allied with the mountain pack. they are not a formal group but a loose network of trappers, exiles, and outlaws who live in the foothills and trade with greyspire, and greyspire alone. they speak a crude sign language to make themselves understood to wolves, and they are universally despised by the other three packs. they are the one faction that deals in objects a wolf can actually carry off (iron points, arrowheads, knives), and the only twolegs any pack calls something close to friends.

**the crow's bargain.** twenty winters ago, a human prospector named **silas crow** stumbled into greyspire territory half-frozen. the highfang of the time, **old grimfang**, spared him in exchange for iron points; arrowheads and knives the wolves learned to use as tools. crow taught greyspire to set ambushes with human technique, communicating through careful gestures and demonstrations. when crow died (of age, in his sleep, a luxury no greyspire wolf has ever known), grimfang buried him in the tooth split. now greyspire wolves lay offerings of fresh-kill at crow's grave before a raid, though the maw's priests call this heresy.
- **the university expedition**: researchers with camera-traps, binoculars, clipboards, and a red-jacketed woman taking creek samples. they want to watch, not to harm; hold still and a "trust event" gets written in their notebook, and meat gets left in return. startle them and they see your face up close, which is its own kind of danger.

the **oak knot** (five points of standing with a faction) marks a real relationship crossing over from tolerance to something like an understanding.

## 6. the cats at the borders

the wolves are not alone in the wild. four **warrior clans** of cats hold their own territories at the caldera's forest edge, living by their own code, and the packs' borders press against theirs:

- **thunderclan**: the oak forest and sunningrocks.
- **shadowclan**: the pine shadows and marsh edge.
- **windclan**: the open moors and rabbit runs.
- **riverclan**: the river, gorge, and reed beds.

the clans meet under truce four times a year at **fourtrees**, a gathering hollow where deputies speak and warriors keep the peace while medicine cats trade herb lore across clan lines. a pack that has forged a **cat pact** has standing at these gatherings; a pack without one watches the season pass without a voice.

relations run on **cat trust**. a wolf's alpha or diplomat can `/pact` to lower border violence and forge understanding; scouts and hunters who cross a clan border carelessly can trigger a live fight with a patrol. clan cats fight quick and low, all claws and maneuvers (forepaw slash, badger defence, rakes), and cannot pin an adult wolf, but they are fast and they do not forget a broken trust. in book one, border paranoia collapses cat trust before the final truce restores it.

## 7. the wild's other teeth

beyond wolves, cats, and twolegs, the caldera is full of things that bite back. the hunt and the patrol are never safe:

- **predators**: coyotes, cougars, bears, wolverines, foxes, and badgers. size decides everything; only a same-size or larger fighter can pin, so a badger is slow but brutal (meet it with the **badger defence**), a fox hits and runs, and a cornered deer or elk can kill a careless hunter.
- **hearth-hounds**: the twolegs' dogs; feral, guard, hunting, and fighting breeds, allied to the human world and dangerous near settlements and work-sites.
- **vermin and reptiles**: water snakes, garter snakes, skinks, and wolf spiders; small, but snake venom is its own slow sentence.
- **the prey**: voles, hares, rabbits, grouse, agouti, beaver, deer, elk, and fish, each with its own moisture and meat. a good kill feeds the den and caches against winter; an empty hunt is a blood debt for a lead hunter.

there is also the **whispering rot**, the swamp's temptation: on a guard's initiation night alone in mistmoor's mud, something in the fog offers betrayal to any wolf who will only listen.

## 8. bones, bonds, and belonging

- **the bones economy**: **bones** are the pack world's currency, earned by hunting, working, praying, crime, and rites, and spent at the ko-fi-free in-game shop on herbs, tools, and toys. a daily stipend and the long rest are the only hard once-a-sunrise limits; almost everything else spends an energy meter instead of being blocked, still working at full value even when energy runs dry (at a cost of exhaustion and mood).
- **bonds**: wolves form tracked relationships that carry real weight; **friendships**, **rivalries**, **kin**, **mentor and apprentice**, **romance**, and **mate** bonds, plus **found families** that gather the unrelated into a den of choice. a fling is auto-detected when a mated pair never forms a romance bond. bonds gate who can adopt, who grieves whom, and how disease spreads along the lines of affection.
- **rogues and loners**: not every wolf holds a pack. **loners** drift the neutral ground; **rogues** like the exiled **splinter** survive by stealing from territory edges out of the **rogue camp**. a wolf cast out past the standing threshold, or by a broken rite, joins them.
- **the drown-sick oracles**: mistmoor's prophets, remade by the belly-rip; and silverrush's **tear-drinkers**, who compare notes on the maw's hunger at the swamp's edge. the caldera's mystics, half-feared and half-needed.

## 10. seasons and omens

the world turns through four seasons, each with real mechanical weight:

- **spring**: herbs are easier to forage; rivers run high and hard to cross. courtship and bonding run year-round.
- **summer**: heat hazards and wildfire smoke; small prey is easier to catch.
- **autumn**: the best hunting; a good kill caches food against the winter.
- **winter**: forage and hunt both turn brutal, hunger falls faster, and the caches from autumn are what keep a den alive. wolves conceive only now.

**omens** ride on the dice. a natural 1 on an omen reading sours the next sunrise; a natural 20 blesses it. a howl that returns the wrong number of echoes, a peak that "bleeds" at dawn, a silence in the belly of the swamp; the packs read all of it, and in book one the omens start coming true.

## 11. illness and the green tongue

illness in howlbert blends real canid ecology with the maw faith. there are over thirty conditions, physical and mental, each with daily penalties to hunger, hydration, hp, hunt yield, or pain exhaustion. every sickness has a **wolf-world name**; the packs do not know human words for the things that eat them. the clinical term is given only so staff can find the mechanic.

### diseases of the body

| wolf-world name | what it is | note |
|---|---|---|
| green-cough → blackcough → redcough | a worsening chest sickness (respiratory infection) | mold spores from rotten meat; deadly if it reaches redcough. |
| yellowcough | a phlegm-heavy lung cough | contagious on the breath. |
| pupcough | the first-winter cough of the very young | can settle into weak lungs for life. |
| leaf-bare cough | the chill-season cough (cold/chill) | milder, but it opens the door to worse. |
| rot-lung | mistmoor's swamp fever in the lungs | the swamp's signature curse; wet, feverish, spreads in the mist. |
| weeping-scale | the crusting, weeping plague (canine distemper) | fever, scabbing, and worse; feared in every den. |
| shiver-fever | the shaking body-fever (influenza) | aches, chills, and a wolf too weak to hunt. |
| river rot | the fouled-gut sickness of poisoned water (from twoleg sewage) | silverrush only; **no known cure**. |
| gut-run (the flux) | violent watery bowels (diarrhea) | dehydrates fast; drink and rest. |
| yellow-eye | the liver-sickness that yellows the gaze (hepatitis) | slow, wasting, hard to read early. |
| the spotting | a blistering skin-and-fever pox (pox) | scabs and misery; runs through a crowded den. |
| scratch-bare | the fur-falling itch (mange) | patches of bald, scabbed skin; a slow shame. |
| the itch | biting parasites in the coat (fleas) | minor alone, a vector for worse. |
| leaf-burn | a rash from a poison plant (poison ivy) | swelling and torment; jewelweed answers it. |
| sting-swell | a venomous insect sting (mild poison) | a hot, swelling bite; usually passes. |
| milk-fever | the nursing-mother's fever (milk fever) | strikes she-wolves with a litter; treat fast. |
| shaking-sickness | uncontrollable tremors and fits (seizure disorder) | the body betraying itself. |
| wasting-sickness | the slow shrinking-away (chronic wasting) | a wolf eating and eating and still fading. |
| growth-sickness | a hidden lump that grows and kills (cancer) | begins silent; the maw's quietest eating. |
| blood-shock | collapse from blood loss (physical shock) | stabilize now or lose them. |
| cloudmouth | the froth-and-madness death (rabies) | **always fatal, no cure**; sedate the wolf far from the pack and wait. |

### sicknesses of the mind

the packs count a wounded mind as real as a wounded leg; each of these is a tracked condition with its own penalties and its own calming herbs.

| wolf-world name | what it is |
|---|---|
| heart-shock | the frozen stillness after trauma (emotional shock). |
| the hollowing | grief that empties a wolf out (grief / melancholy). |
| the sleepless | nights that will not release into sleep (insomnia). |
| the skitters | a body wired to bolt at nothing (anxiety). |
| fever-wander | mind adrift in fever-dreams (delirium). |
| the long-forgetting | memory unraveling with age (dementia). |
| den-madness | contagious mistrust that turns a pack on itself (paranoia / pack madness). |
| feral-shift | the pull back toward the wild, mindless wolf (feral shift). |
| the fixing | a mind locked on one thing (obsession). |
| the screaming-sleep | dreams that wake the whole den (night terrors). |
| the fraying | a wolf worn thin by unending strain (chronic stress). |
| the refusing | a belly that will not take food (eating distress). |

### herbs

over a hundred herbs with real properties, gathered by foragers, kept in the healers' den store, and worked by medics into poultices, draughts, and surgery. some herbs heal; some are poison held one leaf from mercy. wolves cannot perform miracles; they set what bones they can, stitch what they can reach, and lose the rest to the maw.

**the menders** (wounds, bones, infection): comfrey, marsh-mallow root, goldenrod, horsetail, yarrow, cobwebs (to clot), coneflower and burdock root (against infection), oak bark and alder bark (astringents), bindweed vines and straight sticks (to splint).

**the pain-and-sleep herbs** (used carefully; they build tolerance and withdrawal): willow bark and meadowsweet (pain), poppy seeds (deep pain, addictive), valerian, chamomile, lavender, passionflower, and dried skullcap (calm, sleep, and the sicknesses of the mind).

**the gut, breath, and fever herbs**: mullein, lungwort, coltsfoot, and pine needles (breath and cough); watermint, chamomile, and blackberry (gut); feverfew and borage (fever and heart); honey (a sweetener that eases starving pups and carries other herbs).

**fur, skin, and pests**: jewelweed (leaf-burn), mugwort and garlic mustard and knotgrass (to ward fleas and biting insects), lamb's ear and dock (soothing dressings).

**the poisons and mercy-herbs** (restricted; misuse maims or kills): deathberries (yew), deadly nightshade, water hemlock, foxglove, oleander, wolfsbane, bloodroot, holly berries, wintergreen, the death-cap mushroom, and mistmoor's own belly-rip fungus. these end pain, end pregnancies, or end lives; the healer's darkest knowledge.

**pack starters** (what each den learns first): greyspire begins with arnica, coneflower, prickly ash, and edelweiss; mistmoor with elderberry, lizard's tail, boneset, and swamp milkweed; thistlehide with comfrey, plantain, oak bark, and slippery elm; silverrush with meadowsweet, alder bark, cattail, and purple loosestrife.

herbs run from **common** (found on most forages) up through **uncommon**, **rare**, and **very rare**, with a separate **restricted** tier for the poisons a medic is trusted, or forbidden, to touch.

## 12. book one: the blinking

the current arc. a wrongness spreads through the caldera in twelve phases, advanced by roleplay rather than a day counter. the mountain bleeds, the warm river rots its fish early, the swamp goes silent, and the packs turn on each other and on the cats at their borders before the truce is finally remembered.

**the shape of it (mechanical pressure, not a fixed script):**

| phase | title | the pressure |
|-------|-------|--------------|
| 1 | bitten moon | a pall over every wolf; moods sink. |
| 2 | white omen | thistlehide's scouts read the first sign. |
| 3 | peak bleeds | the mountain turns deadly to cross; greyspire hunts well. |
| 4 | warm below | silverrush's river warms; fish rot; wolves go thirsty. |
| 5 | belly silence | mistmoor's rot-lung presses; the swamp holds its breath. |
| 6 | border paranoia | trust with the cats collapses; border fights spike. |
| 7 | iron debt | the forest turns hard to travel; old debts come due. |
| 8 | mill tooth | the twoleg mill gives up a fossil tooth. |
| 9 | memory bites | a fractured pack pays for its disunity in the howl. |
| 10 | blame spiral | every pack frays at once; the cats pull away. |
| 11 | ash naming | the naming that begins to heal it; howls knit unity back. |
| 12 | pact remembered | the truce returns; the warm-river curse breaks. |

**the splinter lane**: an exiled greyspire wolf, **splinter**, works the territory edges through the arc; a thief and a symptom, not the mastermind. redemption or death stays in the hands of roleplay and combat.

**who carries it**: every wolf can take part (first plot-tagged action each sunrise lifts mood, sniff and healer quests progress the arc). the canon anchors carry extra weight: thistlehide's healer apprentice **firepaw** and mistmoor's healer **soot** run the healer lanes; **river'shroud** and **finnpelt** hold thistlehide's leadership beats.

## 14. glossary of special terms

the words the packs use for their world. disease and herb names live in their own sections above.

### the world and the faith
- **the caldera**: the collapsed crater where all four territories meet
- **the tongue-stone**: the flat black slab at the caldera's center that stays warm in winter; sacred, unowned ground.
- **the twilight zone**: the ring of neutral ground around the tongue-stone, where the packs meet under truce.
- **the echo**: the way a howl returns three times over the caldera; the third echo "belongs to the maw."
- **the moon-howl**: the wolf-only full-moon gathering at the tongue-stone (offering, bone-draw, five-breath report).
- **the maw**: the packs' god; a wounded, hungry thing that "chews." neither good nor evil, but it keeps count.
- **maw favor**: standing earned with the maw through prayer, rites, and offerings.
- **karma**: the maw's tally of a wolf's cruelty and sin; enough of it draws divine attention, then divine wrath.
- **the belly-rip**: the sucking sinkhole in mistmoor's swamp; the maw's mouth in the world.
- **drown-sick**: a wolf remade prophetic and riddling after surviving a fall into the belly-rip.
- **tear-drinker**: a silverrush mystic who reads the maw's hunger at the water's edge.
- **the maw's mouth**: the contested river site held during the rite of broken canine.

### the packs and their ranks
- **great pack**: one of the four major packs (greyspire, mistmoor, thistlehide, silverrush).
- **path**: the guiding way of a pack; the teeth, the belly, the fur, the tears.
- **highfang**: greyspire's alpha; rule by strength.
- **stoneguard**: greyspire's warrior-and-guard caste.
- **lowbelly**: greyspire's lowest rank; the bottom of the pile.
- **rotmother**: mistmoor's alpha; rule by patience and dreams.
- **the current**: silverrush's alpha; the seat that "turns" like the river.
- **the seat**: any pack's alpha position.
- **den**: a pack's home ground, and the pack itself.

### living, dying, and belonging
- **sunrise**: one in-game day; the daily rollover when needs decay and cooldowns reset.
- **fresh-kill**: freshly killed prey; the fresh-kill pile is the den's shared food.
- **bones**: the pack world's currency, earned by hunting, working, praying, and rites.
- **the blooding**: a juvenile's solo kill to earn an adult role.
- **rite of broken canine**: the only challenge that can unseat a sitting alpha.
- **wind that never howled**: a pup who died before being named; unremembered.
- **the weep stone**: silverrush's site of solitary grief.
- **the whisper tree**: thistlehide's ancestor-tree that answers questions about the forest.
- **found family**: a den of choice, binding unrelated wolves.
- **loner / rogue**: a packless wolf; rogues (like splinter) survive by theft from the edges.
- **the green tongue**: the whole body of herb-lore and the healers who carry it.
- **the healer's code**: the medic's rules; neutral scent, cannot refuse the sick, punished for secret mates or pups.

### the world outside
- **twoleg**: a human.
- **thunderpath**: a road; the black river of twoleg machines.
- **thunderstick**: a gun.
- **hearth-hound**: a twoleg's dog (feral, guard, hunting, or fighting).
- **the crows**: greyspire's human allies (people, not birds); a loose network of trappers, exiles, and outlaws in the foothills who trade only with greyspire, named after **silas crow**.
- **silas crow**: the twoleg prospector old grimfang spared twenty winters ago in exchange for iron tools; taught greyspire human ambush technique and is buried in the tooth split, where wolves leave fresh-kill offerings before raids.
- **the crow's bargain**: greyspire's alliance with the crows; iron tools and ambush-craft for the pack, in exchange for peace and trade.
- **oak knot**: five points of standing with a twoleg faction; the mark of a real understanding.
- **the clans**: the four warrior-cat clans at the forest borders (thunderclan, shadowclan, windclan, riverclan).
- **fourtrees**: the hollow where the cat clans gather under truce.
- **cat pact / cat trust**: a pack's treaty and standing with the clans.
- **the whispering rot**: the swamp's voice that tempts a lone guard toward betrayal on their initiation night.
