# staff rules

being on the staff team means holding the den together. staff are the wolves who keep the space safe,
fair, and running, so the bar is higher for us than for anyone else, not lower. these rules sit on top
of the normal server rules; where the two ever seem to disagree, this doc and the server rules both
win over convenience, and the owner has the final call.

**the staff team:** owner, co-owners, moderator, and trial moderator (a probation period before full
moderator). age verification is handled by a named, trusted subset of staff (verifiers); see the
verification section. staff roles are jobs of service, not ranks of power.

---

## the staff roles (what each does)

at this size the roles overlap a lot and everyone pitches in; these are the leans, not hard walls.

**owner (basil).** runs and maintains howlbert itself (the bot lives on the owner's machine, so
hosting, updates, bug fixes, and new features are the owner's job), sets the lore, plot, rules, and
direction, and has the final call on everything. handles the heaviest decisions: bans, partnerships,
staff hiring and removal, safety escalations, and the ko-fi and kickstarter side.

**co-owners.** the owner's seconds, and the people who can act with owner-level authority when the
owner is away. they share the big calls (bans, partnerships, staff decisions, safety), back up
escalations, and help steer the community day to day, and are trusted with sensitive matters like
verification oversight and serious disputes. **some co-owners also hold the bot admin commands**;
those who do are bound by the bot-power rules in section 5 exactly like the owner.

**moderator.** the day-to-day of keeping the den fair and tidy: enforcing the rules, handling reports
and tickets, warning, muting, or kicking per the consequences ladder, welcoming and onboarding new
members, answering questions, and watching RP for godmodding, metagaming, and powerplaying. mediates
consent and PvP disputes, helps run events and keep chat alive, and escalates the big calls (bans,
safety, partners) up the chain. may serve as a verifier.

**trial moderator.** a moderator on probation, learning the ropes and proving reliability before
becoming a full moderator. does the same day-to-day work with **lighter powers**: can warn and handle
routine issues, but the heavier actions (kicks, bans) go through a full moderator or the owner first.

**verifier (a duty, not a rank).** a small, trusted set of staff (the owner, co-owner, and select
moderators) who run the age-verification checks and grant access once a member is confirmed. bound by
the verification and confidentiality rules in sections 6 and 9.

---

## 1. lead by example

* staff are held to the **same server rules as everyone else**. a staff tag is not immunity; it is a
  reason to model the behavior we ask for.
* the NSFW, AI, harassment, and safety rules apply to staff in every channel and DM, no exceptions.
* if a staff member breaks the rules, it is treated as more serious, not less, because of the trust
  the role carries.

## 2. service, not power

* staff positions exist to **help the community**, not to sit above it. we handle the lights; we do
  not need to be in the spotlight.
* do not use a staff role, or the implication of one, to win arguments, pressure members, shape IC
  (in-character) outcomes, or intimidate anyone.

## 3. equal treatment, no favoritism

* in anything that matters (rule breaks, applications, plot decisions, disputes), **all members are
  treated equally**, regardless of their friendship or standing with staff.
* decisions are made on merit and the server guidelines, never on personal bias.
* **recuse yourself** when you have a conflict: do not moderate a dispute involving a close friend, a
  partner, your own pack, or anyone you have a personal or romantic interest in. hand it to another
  staff member.

## 4. no auto-wins in roleplay

* staff characters get **no special treatment in RP**. they face the same growth, risks,
  consequences, and **permanent death** as any other wolf.
* staff must consent to major PvP and death-risk scenes like everyone else, and cannot use their
  position to escape a lost roll or a consequence.

## 5. bot and admin powers

howlbert gives staff real power over a permadeath game with a real economy. **that power is for
running the game, never for personal or IC gain.**

* **never use admin commands to benefit yourself, your wolves, or your friends.** that includes
  `/wolfadmin` (`assign`, `transfer`, `possess`, `release`, `healall`, `execute`, `dormant`,
  `approve`) and `/patronadmin item`, among others.
* specifically: **no** granting yourself or anyone bones, items, or rewards; **no** healing, reviving,
  or resurrecting a wolf that lost; **no** `execute` outside a logged, agreed moderation action; **no**
  `possess`-ing another player's wolf except for a genuine, necessary moderation reason, and never to
  act out a scene or change an outcome.
* death is permanent for everyone, including us. admin powers do not undo a death, a disease, a wound,
  or a bad roll for a staff member or a friend.
* admin actions should be **transparent to the owner**. if you are unsure whether an admin action is
  appropriate, ask first.

## 6. age verification and confidential data

verifiers handle sensitive things, so this is strict.

* **never record, screenshot, save, or forward** a verification call, a photo ID, a face, or any
  document. confirm, then let it go. nothing is kept.
* keep every verification **confidential**. do not discuss who verified how, or share anything a
  member showed you, with anyone outside the verifier team.
* **recuse** from verifying anyone you have a personal or romantic interest in; hand it to another
  verifier.
* never pressure a member on camera or push for more than the process needs. if a member is
  uncomfortable, offer the private alternative.
* verification exists for the **safety and comfort** of the community, not to collect anyone's data.

## 7. conflict of interest and IC rank

* **high IC ranks and staff roles.** both a staff role and a high IC rank (alpha, advisor, lead medic)
  take real time and attention, and holding both is a conflict of interest and a burnout risk. **while
  the server is still small, staff may hold a high IC rank out of necessity**, since there are not
  enough players to fill every seat. the expectation is that **staff step back from high IC ranks as
  the server grows** and the community can fill those seats. either way, a staff member who holds a
  high IC rank must **recuse** from any moderation touching their own rank, pack, or the players under
  them. the game already keeps one player's wolves in separate packs to prevent conflict of interest
  and metagaming; this is the same principle, applied as the server can afford it.
* high IC ranks that members could earn are **not a staff perk**. do not claim opportunities out from
  under the community.
* staff do not **approve their own** applications, plots, or feature requests; another staff member
  reviews them.

## 8. test and admin accounts

* admin or test wolves (used to check features) **never appear on leaderboards or touch the economy**,
  are clearly marked, and are **deleted after testing**. the test wolf never becomes a real advantage.
* do not use a test wolf to seed bones, items, or standing onto a real account.

## 9. confidentiality

* staff see tickets, reports, DMs, verification, and sometimes members at their worst. **none of it
  gets leaked, screenshotted out, or gossiped about.**
* server-exclusive lore and plot plans shared with staff stay with staff until they are meant to be
  public.

## 10. enforce the rules evenly

* apply the AI, NSFW, and safety rules the **same for everyone**, including partners, friends, and
  fellow staff. a rule we do not enforce is not a rule.
* if a partner server or its staff break our rules in our space, they are held to them like anyone
  else.

## 11. handling reports and escalation

* take rule breaks and reports to the staff team **privately**, not to general chat. never argue a
  moderation decision in public.
* when in doubt, or when a call is big (a ban, a serious safety issue, a partner problem),
  **escalate to the owner** rather than acting alone.
* disagreements between staff are worked out privately and respectfully; the owner has the final call.

## 12. activity, burnout, and stepping down

* staff are expected to be **reasonably active** and to help when the team is stretched. if you go
  quiet without a word for a long stretch, the role may be reassigned; this is not a punishment, it is
  keeping the team working.
* **communicate**. if life gets busy or you need a break, tell the team and take the hiatus; we would
  rather protect you from burning out than lose you.
* stepping down is always okay and never held against you. return any elevated roles and powers
  cleanly when you go.

## 13. consequences for staff

* **a talk and a warning:** minor lapses, a missed responsibility, a first small mistake.
* **removal from the staff team:** repeated favoritism, ignoring conflicts of interest, misuse of
  moderation, or breaking member trust.
* **removal plus a ban:** abusing admin or bot powers for gain, leaking or misusing verification data
  or private reports, harassment, or any serious safety violation. the more trust a role holds, the
  harder the fall.

---

staff keep this a place people feel safe creating in. lead with that, ask when unsure, and treat the
role as something you hold for the community, not over it.
