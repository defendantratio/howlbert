# NPC

**Name:** Yarrow  
**Pack:** Mistmoor  
**Rank:** Scout  
**Age:** 2 years  
**Sexuality:** Bisexual  
**Gender/Pronouns:** Male (he/him)

**Appearance:**  
Yarrow is a small, wiry wolf with a coat of pale green-gray and cream — like the underside of a lily pad. His fur is short and slick, and he is always damp. His eyes are a bright, curious green, and he has a small, white star on his forehead. His ears are large and floppy, and his tail is short and blunt. He moves with a quick, darting gait, always looking over his shoulder.

**Personality:**  
*Eager, nervous, talkative, loyal, easily startled.*  

Yarrow is the youngest scout in Mistmoor, and he is terrified of failing. He checks his patrol routes twice, reports three times, and apologizes constantly. Older wolves find him annoying. He knows. He cannot stop.

He is talkative — too talkative — sharing every detail of every patrol, whether anyone wants to hear it. He is working on being quieter. He is not very good at it.

**Backstory:**  
Yarrow was born in Mistmoor to a Bog-Born mother and a scout father. His mother died of rot-lung when he was a pup. His father is still alive — a scout named Reedstalk.

His father is harsh and demanding, pushing Yarrow to be better, faster, quieter. Yarrow fears disappointing him more than anything.

**Skills:**
- **Stealth (Dexterity +4):** Surprisingly quiet when he remembers to shut up.
- **Tracking (Intelligence +3):** Sharp nose; can follow a trail through mud and water.
- **Survival (Wisdom +3):** Knows which mushrooms are edible and which will kill him.

**Weaknesses:**
- **Talks Too Much (Charisma -3):** Gives away his position. Drives other wolves mad.
- **Nervous (Wisdom -3):** Prone to panic attacks, especially during storms.
- **Desperate for Approval (Charisma -3):** Can be manipulated by any wolf who offers praise.

**RP Sample:**  
Yarrow crept through the reeds, his heart pounding. He was supposed to be quiet. He was never quiet.

A twig snapped under his paw. He froze. Listened. Nothing.

“You are so loud,” he whispered to himself.

He kept moving, wincing at every rustle. By the time he reached the border, he had memorized a list of all the sounds he made. He would apologize to the Rotmother later. She would sigh. She always sighed.
