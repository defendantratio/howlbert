[💕] Relationship Rules
**1. IC Relationship Categories**
The server recognizes several types of in-character bonds:
- **Pack Bonds** — The loyalty (or disloyalty) between packmates. The strongest ties in a wolf's life.
- **Friendships** — Voluntary bonds of trust and affection, not bound by blood or rank.
- **Rivalries** — Competitive, antagonistic, or hate-driven relationships. Often as intense as love.
- **Mates** — A bonded pair (or rarely, trio) committed to raising pups and sharing territory. Can be romantic, political, or practical.
- **Flings / Courtship** — Temporary or developing romantic/sexual connections without the full commitment of mating.
- **Family** — Blood relations (parents, siblings, offspring, extended kin).
- **Cross-Pack Ties** — Relationships that cross pack borders. These are taboo but not impossible.

**2. No NSFW Content in Relationships**
- As per the server's core rules, **no sexual, adult, or NSFW roleplay is allowed anywhere** – this includes all relationship dynamics (mating, flings, courtship, or any intimate scenes).
- Romantic intimacy must be portrayed through fade-to-black, implication, or emotional connection – explicit content is strictly prohibited.
- Do not describe physical acts, bodily details, or sexual tension in explicit terms.
- Violations will result in an **immediate ban**.

**3. OOC Relationships vs. IC Relationships**
- Your character's feelings are not your feelings. IC rivalry does not mean OOC hostility.
- IC romance does not require OOC romance. It's collaborative storytelling.
- If OOC feelings interfere with IC dynamics, step back and communicate with moderators.

**4. Mating Rules**
- Mating is an IC commitment, not an OOC contract. Wolves may become mates through ceremony (e.g., the Alpha-chosen pairs), or simply by choosing each other.
- Mates are expected to hunt together, share dens, and defend each other. Breaking a mating bond can have IC consequences (jealousy, exile, blood feuds).
- There is no "marriage" equivalent. Mates stay together for as long as both wish. Separation is allowed but may be messy.

**5. Multiple Mates**
- Wolves are not strictly monogamous. Polyamorous bonds (e.g., a triad of mates) are permitted with all players' consent.
- A wolf may take a secondary mate while keeping a primary mate, but this requires IC negotiation and may cause drama.

**6. Cross-Pack Mating**
- Wolves from different packs becoming mates is allowed but carries heavy IC risks.
- **Greyspire** views cross-pack mates as traitors unless the other wolf joins Greyspire.
- **Mistmoor** may see it as a way to "steal" swamp secrets. Dangerous.
- **Thistlehide** might remember the couple but not trust them.
- **Silverrush** is most tolerant, but still wary.
- Cross-pack mates often become loners, outcasts, or must choose one pack. This is a major plot driver.

**7. Mating Rituals**
Packs have different traditions:
- **Greyspire:** The pair must hunt a dangerous prey together (mountain goat, bear cub) and return with scars to prove their bond.
- **Mistmoor:** The pair drinks from the Belly-Rip's edge (not falling in) and shares a prophetic dream. If dreams align, they are mates.
- **Thistlehide:** The pair howls their ancestors' names together, binding their memories. The pack witnesses and remembers the union.
- **Silverrush:** The pair swims the Ripsnout channel together. If both survive, they are mates. If one drowns, the bond was not meant to be.

**8. Breaking a Mating Bond**
- Mates may separate IC. Reasons include betrayal, death of pups, political necessity, or simply falling apart.
- Separated mates may remain in the same pack (awkward) or one may leave.
- A scorned mate may seek revenge. This is allowed but must follow PvP consent rules.
- No OOC harassment for IC breakups.

**9. Pregnancy & Birth**
- Pregnancy lasts ~63 days IC. You may time-skip or play through the gestation.
- Litters are typically 4–6 pups. You may name and play only 1–3 pups as NPCs or your own characters; the rest are assumed to be NPCs who may survive or die as plot demands.

**10. Playing Pups**
- Pups (under 1 year) can be played as characters but cannot be involved in sexual content (see main rules).
- Pups are vulnerable. They can be killed in moderated events only with player consent.
- Pups grow up over time. **Aging a pup requires moderator approval** and typically occurs during mod-run time skips or plot events. You may not age your pup arbitrarily without staff oversight.

**11. Adoption & Found Family**
- Wolves may adopt orphaned pups, even from other packs. Adoption requires IC consent from the birth parents if they are active characters.
- Found family (non-blood bonds as strong as blood) is encouraged. The Maw values memory, not genetics.

**12. Family Drama**
- Sibling rivalries, parental favoritism, and inheritance disputes are highly encouraged.
- Patricide/matricide is allowed but must follow combat/death rules (player consent or mod event).
- Family secrets (e.g., "your father was actually from Mistmoor") are great plot hooks.

**13. Spies & Traitors**
- A wolf may betray their pack to another pack. This is a major plot point.
- The betraying player must OOC inform moderators before acting.
- Consequences are severe (exile, death, or being forgotten).
- No player may be forced to make their character a traitor.

**14. Major NPC Relationships**
- NPCs (run by moderators) may serve as mates, parents, rivals, or litter-mates for your character.
- These relationships can be developed through events.
- NPC deaths are not subject to player consent (they're NPCs), but mods will handle them fairly.

**15. Minor NPCs**
- You may create minor NPCs (e.g., an unnamed packmate, a background elder) without approval.
- Minor NPCs cannot be used to give your character unfair advantages (e.g., "my NPC friend gives me extra food every day").
- Minor NPCs can be killed or removed by mods as needed.

**16. Human Relationships**
- Wolves may form relationships with specific humans (e.g., the Harrow Daughter, Silas Crow historically).
- These are complex and often tragic. Humans are not playable characters, but human NPCs exist.
- Romantic/sexual relationships with humans are not allowed (||bestiality|| themes are prohibited).

**17. Dark Themes**
- No glorification of abuse. It should be portrayed as tragic or villainous.

**18. Shipping & Pressure**
- No pressuring other players to ship their characters with yours. "Shipping" is collaborative, not coercive.
- If someone says "no" to a romantic plotline, accept it gracefully. Don't ask why. Just move on.
- Public shipping channels or memes about IC couples are fine as long as everyone involved is comfortable.

**19. Arranged Mating & Forced Bonds**
- Alphas may arrange matings for political reasons (e.g., to strengthen alliances, settle disputes).
- Players must **OOC consent** before their character is placed in an arranged bond. No forced IC mating without OOC agreement.
- Arranged mates may grow to love each other, resent each other, or simply tolerate each other – all are valid arcs.

**20. Casual Physical Intimacy**
- Flings, casual courtship, and brief romantic encounters are allowed.
- All parties must **OOC consent** before any intimate IC scene (even kissing, cuddling, or sharing a den overnight).
- No assumptions – ask first, even if it's "just a fling."

**21. Mentor / Protégé Bonds**
- A specific bond outside family and pack: an older or more experienced wolf may take on a student or guide.
- This creates a power dynamic that should be respected IC. The mentor holds influence, and the protégé may be expected to follow guidance.
- Mentor bonds can evolve into friendship or rivalry, but **romance is strictly prohibited** due to the inherent power imbalance and the potential for OOC discomfort.
- If a mentor-protégé dynamic shifts in a way that feels inappropriate, players are encouraged to discuss it OOC and, if needed, involve staff.

**22. Handling Jealousy (IC vs. OOC)**
- IC jealousy is allowed and encouraged for drama – it fuels conflict and storytelling.
- **OOC jealousy** must be kept separate. If you feel jealous of another player's IC relationship, step back, breathe, and talk to a mod if needed.
- Do not use IC jealousy as an excuse for OOC hostility or passive-aggressive behavior.

**23. Reconciliation & Re-mating**
- Broken bonds (mating, friendship, or family) may be mended over time.
- Reconciliation requires IC scenes showing the effort to rebuild trust, and **mutual player consent**.
- Re-mating after a breakup is allowed but should not be rushed – let the story breathe.

**24. Grief & Loss**
- Losing a mate, pup, family member, or close friend is a major IC event.
- Players should allow their characters to grieve realistically – anger, withdrawal, depression, or seeking revenge are all valid.
- Do **not** rush a grieving player's character to "get over it." Respect their pacing.
- Grief can be a powerful motivator for character growth or villain arcs.

**25. Hybrid Pups (Cross-Pack Offspring)**
- Pups born from cross-pack mates are often viewed with suspicion by both packs.
- They may be claimed by either pack, rejected by both, or born as outcasts.
- Hybrid pups' status (which pack they belong to, if any) requires staff approval and should be handled IC with care.
- These pups often face unique challenges – identity, belonging, and loyalty conflicts.
