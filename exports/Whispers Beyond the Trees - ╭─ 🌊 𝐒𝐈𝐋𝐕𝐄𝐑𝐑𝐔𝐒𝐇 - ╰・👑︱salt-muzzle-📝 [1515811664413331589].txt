# NPC

**Name:** Saltmuzzle  
**Pack:** Silverrush  
**Rank:** Alpha (Current)  
**Age:** 7 years  
**Sexuality:** Heterosexual  
**Gender/Pronouns:** Female (she/her)

**Appearance:**  
Saltmuzzle is a striking wolf with a coat the color of river stones: pale gray, silver, and white, with darker speckles across her shoulders. Her fur is short and sleek, perfect for swimming, and she has webbed paws (a rare genetic trait in Silverrush). Her eyes are a deep, calm blue, and she has a habit of tilting her head when she listens, as if she is hearing something far away. Her left ear is torn in two places (battle scars from a Greyspire raid years ago).

**Personality:**  

Saltmuzzle has led Silverrush for three years, since the previous Current drowned in a flash flood. She did not want the role: she was a diplomat, a peacemaker, a wolf who hated violence. But the pack chose her. She leads the only way she knows how: with patience, with negotiation, with a steady voice that does not waver.

But she is tired. The river’s warmth, the mill, the packs circling each other: it is all too much. She misses the old days, when the biggest problem was a flooded den or a stolen kill. She wonders if she is the right wolf for this moment. She does not say this aloud.

**Backstory:**  
Saltmuzzle was born in Silverrush, the daughter of a Current who died when she was young. Her mother was a diplomat, and Saltmuzzle followed in her pawsteps: learning the art of negotiation, the language of truce, the weight of a promise.

Her wound came five years ago, when a Greyspire raid killed her mate. He was not a guard, he was a forager, gentle and kind, and he died protecting a litter of pups that were not his. Saltmuzzle watched him fall. She did not scream. She did not fight. She just… carried on. That is what Silverrush wolves do. They carry on.

She has not taken another mate. She does not think she ever will.

**RP Sample:**  
Saltmuzzle stood at the river’s edge, her webbed paws sinking into the wet sand. The water was warm, too warm, and it carried a faint, iron smell. She had been standing here for a quarter-moon, watching the current, listening to its voice.

Aromis approached from behind. “Current. The patrol returned.”

Saltmuzzle did not turn. “What did they find?”

“The tooth. In the mill. They brought back a piece.”

Saltmuzzle closed her eyes. She had hoped, foolishly, that the tooth was a story. A legend. Something to frighten pups.

“Bring it to me,” she said. “And then send word to the other packs. We need to talk.”

Aromis hesitated. “The other packs? After everything?”

Saltmuzzle turned. Her blue eyes were calm, but there was something hard beneath them.

“Especially after everything.”

**Other:** Open to plots where she must choose between peace and war, or where her mate’s death is avenged.
