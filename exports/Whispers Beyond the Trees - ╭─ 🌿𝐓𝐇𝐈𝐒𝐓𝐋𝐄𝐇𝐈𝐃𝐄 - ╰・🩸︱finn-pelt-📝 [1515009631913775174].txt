**Appearance:**  
Finn’pelt is a hulking, broad-shouldered wolf built like an unyielding stone wall, anchoring any clearing he steps into. His name comes from his incredibly thick, ash-black coat, which is heavily slicked with natural river-oils, causing thorns, brambles, and enemy teeth to slide right off it like armor plating. His expression is defined by a cold, unblinking stare from intense golden eyes, with a single jagged, white scar cutting vertically through his right eye — though his vision miraculously remains flawless.

**Personality:**  
Vigilant, stoic, strict, protective, emotionally oblivious.  

Finn’pelt is a pillar of steady, rugged authority. He does not rule through explosive rage or tyrannical cruelty, but through an absolute, crushing reliability that demands instant respect. When he enters a space, the pack naturally drops their heads out of an instinctual recognition of safety and power. He believes a pack can only survive the harshness of the Maw if every gear turns perfectly, making him incredibly strict regarding duties and borders.

Beneath his hard, militaristic exterior lies a deep, quiet kindness. He cannot offer comforting words or emotional validation, but he shows his devotion through heavy, silent actions. He will stay awake for days patrolling the hostile Greyspire border so the younger hunters can sleep, or secretly leave the choice cuts of a kill at the entrance of a nursing mother's den. He breaks his own heart enforcing the pack’s dark rules because he genuinely believes it is the only way to save Thistlehide from extinction.

**Backstory:**  
Finn’pelt was born to a high-ranking Thistlehide warrior during a season of bitter starvation. From the moment he could walk, the harsh laws of the pack were pressed into his mind: the weak drag down the whole, and the Alpha’s word is absolute law. He grew up watching the older generations give their bodies to the Maw, conditioning him to see life not as a gift, but as a duty of endurance. His early life was defined by the constant, grinding pressure of the Greyspire border conflict, where he learned that hesitation meant a shallow grave.

His defining Wound came during a brutal skirmish in the high valley over a critical water runoff. Finn’pelt was ambushed by a Greyspire vanguard, receiving the terrible scar across his eye and nearly having his throat torn out. Instead of fleeing, his absolute refusal to break allowed him to hold the line alone until reinforcements arrived. This act of sheer physical willpower earned him the name Finn’pelt, celebrating his armor-like resilience. When he later challenged the previous Alpha in the Rite of Broken Canine, he did not kill his opponent; he shattered the old wolf's jaw, asserted absolute authority, and spared him to serve as his "Living Prisoner" advisor.

A year into his rule, Finn’pelt encountered the strange, terrifying constructs of Twolegs near the edge of the territory. He watched a massive, roaring iron beast tear through the ancient trees, leaving a bleeding swath of mud and oil in its wake. While other wolves panicked, Finn’pelt stood his ground, observing how the humans moved and identifying their reliance on their strange, booming thunder-sticks. He strictly forbade his pack from hunting near the human paths, marking them as an unnatural hazard that required absolute vigilance to avoid.

**RP Sample:**  
The fog rolling off the high valley was thick enough to choke on, tasting of wet slate and old blood. Finn’pelt stood motionless on the rocky precipice, his ash-black fur beaded with heavy droplets of condensation. Below him, the border to Greyspire territory was a jagged scar across the earth. His golden eyes narrowed, scanning the treeline for any sign of silver or ash-colored coats. Every muscle in his massive frame was coiled, waiting, acting as the living wall his pack required him to be.

A low rustle in the briars behind him broke the silence. A young, trembling hunter slid out of the brush, ears pinned back in exhaustion. "Alpha... the Greyspire patrol, they shifted further west. We lost the scent tracking them near the rocks." The young wolf braced for a reprimand, lowering his chest to the damp earth.

Finn’pelt did not turn his head, his gravelly voice cutting through the mist like a blunt blade. "Get back to the dens and eat. I will hold the ridge until the sun breaks." He did not look down at the subordinate, his low Wisdom failing to register the deep, shivering terror and exhaustion the young wolf carried. He only knew the border was vulnerable. As the hunter scrambled away in relief, Finn’pelt stepped deeper into the cold wind, his armored coat absorbing the chill, ready to bleed for the dirt he ruled.
