### Injury Table (1d10)
Roll 1d10 whenever a wolf drops to 0 HP or takes a critical hit:

1. **Broken tooth** — Disadvantage on bite attacks. No cure — permanent.
2. **Torn claw** — −1 to claw damage. Heals in 1 week, or 3 days with horsetail poultice.
3. **Deep gash** — Bleeding: lose 1 HP/hour until bandaged. Yarrow + cobwebs stops bleeding.
4. **Sprained leg** — Movement halved. Comfrey + 3 days rest.
5. **Fractured rib** — Disadvantage on Strength checks; cannot hunt. Comfrey poultice + 2 weeks rest.
6. **Concussion** — Disadvantage on Intelligence checks; memory loss. Dried skullcap + 1 week sleep.
7. **Punctured paw** — Disadvantage on Dexterity checks involving running or climbing. Heals in 1 week; binding with oak bark halves time.
8. **Infected wound** — Roll 1d20 + Survival/Constitution each morning. On a failure, lose 1 HP and gain 1 exhaustion level until treated with yarrow or goldenrod poultice.
9. **Torn ear / lost eye** — Permanent −1 to Perception (Wisdom) checks. No cure if eye is lost; torn ear heals cosmetically but penalty remains.
10. **Broken jaw** — Cannot bite or eat solid food. Requires liquid diet (broth, milk) and 3 weeks rest. Comfrey poultice reduces to 2 weeks.

## GRAPPLE MECHANICS

**Grapple:** Opposed Strength vs. Strength roll. A successful pin reduces the target's available actions.

**Pinned Effects:**

- Icefang is pinned. She cannot move or stand.
-  Icefang attacks with disadvantage (if she tries to bite or claw from the ground).
- River'Shroud has advantage on attacks against Icefang while she is pinned.
- Icefang cannot use her movement until she breaks the grapple.
- Breaking the grapple: On Icefang's turn, she can use her action to attempt another opposed Strength check to escape.


## NARRATIVE EXAMPLE

Icefang thrashes beneath River'Shroud, her filed teeth snapping. But the massive Thistlehide hunter is too heavy, too solid. Each attempt to twist free is met with more weight pressing down on her chest.

River'Shroud's pale gray eyes bore into Icefang's flat blue stare. A low rumble vibrates through her chest — not a growl, but a question. *Yield?*

Icefang's tongue still throbs from where she bit it. She cannot speak. But even if she could, she would not yield.

She lies still, waiting for an opening that may never come.


## WHAT HAPPENS NEXT?

- **Icefang's next turn:** She can attempt to escape the grapple using her action.
- **If she stays pinned:** River'Shroud can attack her with advantage next turn.
- **If Finn'pelt or Grim step in:** The fight could shift.

## GM NOTE ON FAILED HERB PREPARATION

When a herb preparation fails, the GM has several options:

1. **The herb is wasted** — As here. The celandine was too old and dry.
2. **The herb is partially effective** — If the DC was missed by only 1-2 points, the GM may rule half effect.
3. **The herb is harmful** — The treatment fails entirely.

**In this case:** The celandine was too old. Hemlock will need to forage for fresh celandine if she wants to reduce Icefang's recovery time.
