# NPC

**Name:** Slate  
**Pack:** Greyspire  
**Rank:** Hunter  
**Age:** 4 years  
**Sexuality:** Heterosexual  
**Gender/Pronouns:** Male (he/him)

**Appearance:**  
Slate is a compact, muscular wolf with a coat of dark gray and charcoal — like a storm cloud over the peaks. His fur is thick and coarse, often dusted with snow. His eyes are a pale, flinty gray, and he has a deep scar across his left shoulder (from a mountain goat’s horn). His tail is short and bushy, and his ears are heavily notched. He moves with a low, ground-eating lope, efficient and silent.

**Personality:**  
*Patient, competitive, grumpy, reliable, secretly soft.*  

Slate has been a hunter for three years and has never missed a kill. He is not flashy — he does not brag or posture. He simply finds prey, stalks it, and brings it down. Other hunters respect him. They do not like him. He is too quiet, too intense, too focused.

He is competitive with Ironjaw, though he would never admit it. He wants to be the best hunter in Greyspire, and Ironjaw is the only wolf who challenges him. He trains in secret, pushing himself harder.

**Backstory:**  
Slate was born to a Stoneguard father and a hunter mother. His mother taught him to hunt; his father wanted him to fight. He chose hunting because it meant spending more time alone in the mountains.

When he was two, he was gored by a mountain goat during a hunt. He dragged himself back to the den with the goat’s horn still embedded in his shoulder. The healer saved his life, but the scar remains. He has hunted goats ever since, as if trying to prove he is not afraid.

**RP Sample:**  
Slate crouched on a ledge, his dark fur blending with the shadow of the cliff. Below him, a herd of goats grazed on a narrow slope. He watched them for an hour, counting their movements, waiting for the straggler.

A goat broke from the herd. Slate moved — not fast, but *smooth*. He dropped onto the goat’s back, jaws closing on its throat. The goat kicked, but Slate held. When it went still, he dragged it back toward the den, his shoulder aching with every step.

Ironjaw met him at the entrance. “Good kill.”

Slate grunted and kept walking. He did not need praise. He needed the ache to stop.
