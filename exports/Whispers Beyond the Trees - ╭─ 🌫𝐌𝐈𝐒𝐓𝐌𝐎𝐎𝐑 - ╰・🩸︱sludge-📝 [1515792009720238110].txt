# NPC

**Name:** Sludge  
**Pack:** Mistmoor  
**Rank:** Hunter  
**Age:** 4 years  
**Sexuality:** Heterosexual  
**Gender/Pronouns:** Male (he/him)

**Appearance:**  
Sludge is a low-slung, heavy-bodied wolf with a coat the color of stagnant pond water: muddy brown, greenish-gray, and black streaked like oil. His fur is permanently matted with wet clay, which dries into cracked plates that flake off when he moves. His eyes are a pale, murky yellow, set deep in a broad, flat skull. One of his hind legs is slightly shorter than the other (a birth defect), giving him a rolling, side-to-side gait that looks clumsy on land but makes him nearly silent in shallow water. His teeth are stained dark brown from chewing rotten wood to mask his scent.

**Personality:**   

Sludge does not chase. He waits. He has been known to lie motionless in a reed bed for an entire day, submerged up to his nose, waiting for a deer to step within one hare-hop of his jaws. Other wolves find him unsettling: not because he is cruel, but because he is *still*. He does not twitch. He does not blink. He simply *becomes* part of the swamp.

He believes that every successful hunt is a gift from the Maw, and every failure is a punishment for impatience. He prays before eating, pressing his nose to the mud and whispering thanks. He also leaves the first bite of every fresh-kill at the edge of the Belly-Rip, an offering.

**Backstory:**  
Sludge was born with a twisted leg. His mother, a Bog-Born, expected him to die. He did not. He learned to hunt by ambush, using his deformity as an advantage: prey did not hear him coming because he could not run. He simply *appeared* from the mud.

When he was two, he was caught in a Twoleg trap: a rusted iron jaw hidden in the shallows. He chewed through his own paw to escape, but the trap closed on a different paw. He lost two toes on his left forepaw. He still hunts. He still waits.

**Skills:**

- **Stealth (Dexterity +4):** Nearly invisible in shallow water. Can hold completely still for half a day, becoming just another rotting log or shadow in the murk. Prey swims past him; enemies search over him. He strikes when they forget he exists.
- **Hunting (Strength +3):** Ambush specialist. Kills with a single neck-snap from below, surfacing from the water like death itself. His technique is practiced to perfection: one lunge, one crack, one meal. If the first strike fails, however, he has no second.
- **Survival (Wisdom +3):** Knows every sinkhole, quicksand patch, and safe route in Mistmoor. He has mapped the swamp in his head, memorizing which paths hold and which swallow. His pack follows him when the fog rolls in. They trust his feet more than their own.

**Weaknesses:**
- **Twisted Leg (Dexterity -3):** Cannot chase fleeing prey. If the ambush fails, the hunt fails. He is a patient hunter because he has no other choice.
- **Missing Toes (Dexterity -2):** Reduced grip on wet banks. Sometimes slips at the worst moment: during the lunge, the getaway, the scramble up a muddy slope. Two toes on his left forepaw are gone. He compensates with caution, but caution has limits.
- **Superstitious (Wisdom -2):** Will abort a hunt if he sees a bad omen (a crow flying left, a ripple without wind, a frog croaking three times in succession). Other wolves find this frustrating, even dangerous. He cannot explain why the omens matter. He only knows that ignoring them has never ended well.

**RP Sample:**  
Sludge had been in the water since moonrise. His nose was the only thing above the surface: two dark holes in a film of green scum. A deer was drinking at the bank, three deer-lengths away. He had watched it for an hour. It had looked up twice, sniffed the air, seen nothing. The wind was in his favor: he smelled of mud and rot, not wolf.

The deer lowered its head again.

Sludge moved. Not fast, *smooth*. A pawstep. A pause. Another pawstep. The water did not ripple. The mud did not suck.

One hare-hop away.

He exploded from the water, jaws closing on the deer’s throat. The animal kicked, splashed, tried to run,  but Sludge’s weight dragged it down into the shallows. He held. And held. And held.

When the deer went still, he released. He pressed his nose to the mud and whispered, *“Thank you, Maw. I was patient.”*

He dragged the fresh-kill back to the den, limping on his twisted leg, leaving a trail of blood and water.

**Other:** Open to plots where his superstition causes conflict, or where his missing toes fail him at a critical moment.
