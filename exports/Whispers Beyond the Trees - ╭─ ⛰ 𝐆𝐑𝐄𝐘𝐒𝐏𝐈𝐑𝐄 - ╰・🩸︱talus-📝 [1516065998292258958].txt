# NPC

**Name:** Talus  
**Pack:** Greyspire  
**Rank:** Hunter  
**Age:** 3 years  
**Sexuality:** Bisexual  
**Gender/Pronouns:** Female (she/her)

**Appearance:**  
Talus is a lean, long-limbed wolf with a coat of pale gray and white — like shattered rock. Her fur is short and thin, making her look smaller than she is. Her eyes are a bright, curious amber, and she has a small, V-shaped scar on her muzzle (from a fox fight). Her tail is long and expressive, always in motion. She moves with a bouncy, eager energy, never still.

**Personality:**  
*Energetic, curious, impulsive, loyal, easily distracted.*  

Talus is the youngest hunter in Greyspire, and she knows it. She is desperate to prove herself, volunteering for every hunt, running every patrol. She talks constantly — about the weather, the prey, the other packs — and older wolves find her exhausting. She does not notice.

She is easily distracted by shiny rocks, unusual birds, or interesting smells. This has cost her hunts. She is working on it. Slowly.

**Backstory:**  
Talus was born to a Lowbelly mother and an unknown father. Her mother died of illness when Talus was a pup. She was raised by the pack’s caretakers, who fed her last and hit her often. She learned to be fast and quiet — not for hunting, but for survival.

When she was two, she caught a hare that no one else could find. The Stoneguard captain noticed. She was promoted to hunter apprentice. She still feels like an imposter.

**Skills:**
- **Hunting (Dexterity +4):** Fast and agile, specializes in hares, ptarmigan, and small prey.
- **Tracking (Intelligence +3):** Sharp nose; can follow a cold trail.
- **Stealth (Dexterity +3):** Moves quietly on snow and loose rock.

**Weaknesses:**
- **Easily Distracted (Wisdom -2):** Loses focus during long hunts.
- **Desperate to Prove Herself (Wisdom -2):** Takes unnecessary risks.
- **Lowbelly Scars (Charisma -2):** Older wolves still sneer at her origins.

**RP Sample:**  
Talus sprinted across the snow, her pale fur blending with the drifts. A hare darted left; she cut right, jaws snapping. She missed. The hare vanished into a crevice.

“Scat-licker,” she muttered.

She sat down, breathing hard, and watched the crevice. The hare would come out eventually. She could wait. She was good at waiting. Probably.

A shiny rock glinted in the sun. She picked it up. She forgot the hare.
