**Appearance:**  
Sypha is a lean, tan-furred she-wolf with a silken coat that shifts between honey-gold and soft brown depending on the light. She always wears a flower crown woven from wild roses and early-blooming blossoms atop her head — a signature she refuses to abandon, even during leaf-bare. Her eyes are mismatched: one deep sapphire blue, the other warm amber-yellow. Both glow with an almost unsettling gentleness, catching the light at dawn and dusk as if the sun itself lives behind them.

Her fur is immaculately clean and well-kept — unusual for a wolf who spends so much time digging through herb patches. Fallen rose petals often scatter across her pelt, clinging to her like natural ornaments. She smells of pine resin, spring lilies, and the faint sweetness of crushed lavender. When she moves through the forest at moonrise, she seems almost enchanted — a creature of old stories rather than fur and bone.

**Personality:**  
Gentle, polite, charming, quick-witted, profoundly intelligent, playfully mischievous.

Sypha presents herself as a lighthearted, almost frivolous wolf. She tells jokes, plays pranks on her packmates, and laughs easily. Younger wolves sometimes mistake her for unserious — until a wound needs stitching or a poison needs identifying. Then her demeanor shifts: focused, sharp, methodical. She is highly skilled and knows it, though she rarely boasts.


[6/14/2026 5:27 PM] lizardpelt
She is a quick thinker, able to assess multiple outcomes in the time it takes another wolf to blink. This makes her an excellent healer and a surprisingly good strategist. Despite her playful exterior, she is deeply loyal to Thistlehide and would never abandon a packmate in need.

Unlike many wolves in the four packs, Sypha grew up without tragedy. She has never lost a litter, never watched a packmate die under her paws, never been betrayed. This lack of trauma makes her both refreshing and, to some older wolves, unsettling. She does not fully understand grief — but she is eager to learn, to help, to become the healer her pack deserves.

**Backstory:**  
Sypha was born in Thistlehide to a hunter father and a forager mother. Neither expected her to survive: she was small, sickly, with mismatched eyes that some called cursed. But she grew. Slowly, steadily, like a root pushing through stone.

Her gift for herbs revealed itself early. As a pup, she chewed on feverfew leaves to cure her own cough. As a yearling, she saved a litter of pups from rot-lung when the pack’s healer was away. By the time she was two, she had been formally trained by an elder Fur-Giver (healer): a gnarled, half-blind wolf named Old Moss who taught her everything she knows about plants, poison, and patience.

She has never encountered Twolegs up close. She has seen their monsters from a distance: the roaring tree-gulpers, the screaming thunderpaths — but she prefers to travel in the deep woods, far from human roads. She gathers her herbs from the quiet places, the places where the Maw’s fur grows thick and undisturbed.

Her flower crown is not just decoration. Each bloom carries a meaning: rose for healing, lavender for calm, clover for luck. She renews it every morning, weaving new stems into old, so that she always carries a piece of the living forest with her.

She prayed — not aloud, but in her chest, a warm flutter of faith. *"Maw, I was careless. Maw, I was proud. Let me find the den. Let me see my pack again."*

A shape loomed ahead. A tree, one she knew. The old oak with the split trunk, two tail-lengths from the healer’s den. She nearly sobbed with relief.

She stumbled inside, shook the snow from her fur, and began laying out her herbs to dry. Her paws trembled. Her mismatched eyes were wet.

*"Never again,"* she whispered. *"I will mark every path. I will not be lost again."*

Then she saw a young wolf shivering in the corner — a pup with a wounded paw, waiting for her return. She smiled, gentle and warm, and knelt to work.
