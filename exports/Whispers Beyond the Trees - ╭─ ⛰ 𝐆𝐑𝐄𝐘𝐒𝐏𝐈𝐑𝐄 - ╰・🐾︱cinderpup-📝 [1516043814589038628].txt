# NPC

**Name:** Cinderpup  
**Pack:** Greyspire  
**Rank:** Pup  
**Age:** 3 moons  
**Sexuality:** Too young  
**Gender/Pronouns:** Male (he/him)

**Appearance:**  
Cinderpup is a soot-gray ball of fluff with darker stripes along his back and a white-tipped tail. His fur is thick and woolly, typical of mountain pups, and his eyes are a pale, stormy gray. He has a small notch in his left ear from a tumble off a rock ledge; a scar he wears with pride. His paws are oversized even for a Greyspire pup, promising future size. He moves with a clumsy, headlong energy, always tripping over his own feet.

**Personality:**  
*Brave (to the point of foolishness), curious, loud, desperate for approval, secretly afraid of the dark.*  

Cinderpup wants to be a Stoneguard when he grows up. He practices pouncing on pinecones and growling at shadows. He talks constantly — about hunts he has never been on, battles he has never fought, monsters he has never seen. Older wolves find him exhausting. He does not notice. He is too busy trying to impress them.

But at night, when the cave is dark and the wind howls through the mountain, Cinderpup presses close to his mother and pretends he is not trembling. He has nightmares about falling — falling from a cliff, falling into a crevasse, falling forever. He has never told anyone.

**Backstory:**  
Cinderpup was born to a Stoneguard mother and a hunter father, both still alive. He is the only survivor of a litter of four; his littermates died of a sickness during their first moon. His mother does not talk about them. Cinderpup does not ask. He feels their absence like a missing tooth.

He was named Cinderpup because of his gray coat and because he was born during a volcanic ashfall. The elders say that wolves born in ash are touched by the Maw — blessed or cursed, they cannot agree. Cinderpup does not care. He just wants to be strong enough to never fall.

**RP Sample:**  
Cinderpup crouched behind a boulder, his gray fur blending with the rock. Ahead of him, an older pup — a yearling named Flintpaw — was eating a piece of dried meat. Cinderpup’s tail wagged. He pounced.

Flintpaw did not move. Cinderpup bounced off his flank and landed in a heap.

“You’re a vole-snout,” Flintpaw said without looking up.

“I’m practicing!” Cinderpup scrambled to his paws, ears burning. “One day I’ll knock you over. You’ll see.”

Flintpaw snorted. Cinderpup limped back to the den, nursing his bruised shoulder, already planning his next attack.

**Other:**

- **Open plots:** Needs a mentor figure; his fear of heights could be exploited or cured; possible death by falling.
