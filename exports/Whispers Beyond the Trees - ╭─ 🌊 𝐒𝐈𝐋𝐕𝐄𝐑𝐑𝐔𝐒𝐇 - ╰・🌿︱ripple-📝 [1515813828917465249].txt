# NPC

**Name:** Ripple  
**Pack:** Silverrush  
**Rank:** Healer  
**Age:** 4 years  
**Sexuality:** Asexual  
**Gender/Pronouns:** Female (she/her)

**Appearance:**  
Ripple is a slender, graceful wolf with a coat of pale cream and soft gray, like sunlight on shallow water. Her fur is always damp, even when she has not been swimming, and it clings to her lean frame. Her eyes are a pale, translucent green: almost colorless in bright light. She has no visible scars, but she walks with a slight limp (a dislocated hip that healed wrong). Her ears are small and rounded, and she has a habit of flattening them against her head when she is thinking.

**Personality:**  

Ripple is not a healer because she wanted to be. She became a healer because she could not stand to see others suffer. She has a gift for easing pain, not just physical pain, but the deeper kind: grief, fear, loneliness. Wolves come to her den not just for wounds, but for comfort. She gives it freely, asking nothing in return.

She is anxious about the river’s warmth. She knows it means something terrible, something beyond her herbs and poultices. She prays to the Maw every night, pressing her nose to the water, asking for guidance. The Maw does not answer. She keeps praying anyway.

**Backstory:**  
Ripple was born with a dislocated hip: a birth defect that should have killed her. Her mother, a healer, refused to let her die. She spent moons manipulating the joint, massaging the muscles, teaching Ripple to walk despite the pain. By the time Ripple was a year old, she could run, swim, and hunt,  but always with a limp, always with a dull ache.

Her mother died of a heart attack when Ripple was two. Ripple found her body in the herb den, still clutching a bundle of feverfew. She did not howl. She just sat beside her mother and finished grinding the herbs.

**RP Sample:**  
Ripple chewed the willow bark until it was soft, then pressed it into the wound. The young wolf, a Driftwood named Pebble, whimpered but held still.

“Good,” Ripple murmured. “You’re doing good.”

She worked in silence, her pale green eyes focused, her damp fur dripping onto the patient. Behind her, the river burbled. It sounded the same as always. But it felt different. Warmer. Hungrier.

“Healer?” Pebble’s voice was small. “Is the Maw angry at us?”

Ripple paused. She did not know how to answer that. She was not sure the Maw felt anything at all. But she could not say that.

“The Maw is hungry,” she said. “Not angry. There is a difference.”

She tied off the bandage and sat back. “Rest now. I’ll check on you at moonrise.”

Pebble closed his eyes. Ripple stared at the river, watching the steam rise, and wondered if she was lying.
