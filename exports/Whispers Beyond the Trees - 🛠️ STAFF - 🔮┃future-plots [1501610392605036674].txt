**OVERARCHING PLOT** — __BOOK ONE: "THE BLINKING"__
The Season/Time:
Late Autumn, dusk, as the moon rises before the sun has fully set — the hour of the Maw's half-open eye.

1. Sight (What is the first thing they notice?)
The moon rising bruised-purple on the horizon, but it is not round. A crescent-shaped chunk is missing from its lower edge, as if something took a bite.

2. Sound (Specific, not generic.)
The wet, rhythmic click-click-hiss of the Blind Pup's jaws as she works her tongue against the roof of her mouth, a sound she makes only when she is seeing something the rest of them cannot.

3. Smell (Memory or trigger.)
Rotting sweetness, like a belly split open after winter starvation, the scent of the Belly-Rip's breath carried on a wind that should be blowing away from Mistmoor, not toward it.

4. Touch (Temperature or texture.)
The river water, normally cold enough to steal breath, gone inexplicably warm: warm as blood, warm as a wound that has not yet closed.

5. Taste (Often in the air or residue.)
Iron. Copper. The taste of a tongue chewed raw from keeping silent when every instinct screams to howl.

**ACT I: THE SETUP (25%)**

**The Ordinary World**
The four packs exist in fragile, angry equilibrium. Greyspire sharpens its teeth on mountain stone. Mistmoor listens to the Belly-Rip's chewing. Thistlehide tends its memory rituals and watches the logging trucks roll deeper into the woods. Silverrush swims its warm river and collects grief-tolls from the humans crossing the bridge. The Maw is present but distant — a fact of life, like winter or hunger.

The moon waxes and wanes normally. Pups are born with eyes. The river runs cold and clear. The swamp's rot is slow and patient.

Then the moon changes.

**The Inciting Incident**
In Thistlehide, a white pup named Kanami, blind since birth, cursed by the pack's elders, frozen forever in a six-month-old body,  begins to speak of things she cannot possibly know. She describes the stars despite never seeing them. She describes a hole in the sky where a star used to be. She describes the moon chewing.

The pack's alpha, Finn'pelt, a hulking, scarred wolf with a voice like gravel and a heart he keeps locked behind walls of duty, faces pressure from his superstitious wolves to execute the "White Omen" before her curse spreads. His younger sister Mossgaze, a quiet forager who reads the fear in every wolf's posture, watches in silence. She knows what Finn'pelt will do: protect Kanami against all reason, because that is who he is beneath the ice.

Finn'pelt refuses to kill a pack-born wolf. He places Kanami under his personal protection. The pack grumbles but obeys.


[6/12/2026 10:17 PM] lizardpelt
The next morning, a Greyspire scout limps into Thistlehide territory with a message: the mountain's highest peak has sweated blood. Red droplets weeping from cracks in the stone.

Within a week, Silverrush reports the river running red,  not blood, but a bloom of rust-colored algae never seen before. The water is warm. The fish are dying. Skye, a black-and-white dire wolf with mismatched eyes and blue ribbons tied into her fur to hide old scars, serves as a guard for Silverrush's alpha. She watches the river turn and feels something ancient stirring beneath her paws.

Mistmoor sends a Drown-Sick, a wolf who fell into the Belly-Rip and returned, to the other packs with a single, wet whisper: "The chewing has stopped. The Maw is listening instead."


[6/12/2026 10:19 PM] lizardpelt
**The Debate**

Each pack's leader faces the same question: *Do we act, or do we wait?*

Greyspire's alpha wants to raid the human settlements for answers. But leaving the mountains now would leave them vulnerable.

Mistmoor's alpha believes the Maw is hungry for a sacrifice. She considers throwing a wolf into the Belly-Rip — but which one?

Finn'pelt wants to bring the packs together. But his own enforcers accuse him of softness. Mossgaze watches her brother's back, knowing that any sign of weakness will be exploited.

Silverrush's alpha knows the river's warmth is spreading. She must find the source — but that means going upstream into Greyspire territory, past the paper mill.

River'Shroud, the massive, mute hunter of Thistlehide who wears deer antlers around her neck like armor, stands on the border with her foster pup Pale'Step, a tiny, asthmatic white-and-brown pup with pink bows in her fur, abandoned by her birth parents and now fiercely protected by River'Shroud's silent devotion. Pale'Step wheezes in the cold air and insults the other pups to hide her terror. River'Shroud does not speak. She simply lowers her massive head and waits for the order to fight.

Kanami speaks again, louder this time, her sightless eyes turned toward the paper mill: "The Maw is waking because it was put to sleep. Someone made it forget. Someone put iron in its mouth."

**ACT II: THE CONFRONTATION (50%)**

**Rising Action**
Wolves from all four packs begin to investigate the anomalies, often crossing into forbidden territory or forming uneasy alliances.

**Subplots by Pack:**

*Greyspire Subplot: "The Iron Debt"*
The paper mill upstream from Silverrush is owned by the same human company that is clear-cutting Thistlehide's forest. Greyspire has traded with human outlaws for years. Now the outlaws bring news: the mill is digging. Not cutting trees,  digging into a mountain spur that Greyspire considers sacred.

Conflict: Greyspire's alpha wants to ally with Silverrush to attack the mill. But Silverrush's wolves are swimmers, not climbers. And the mountain wolves refuse to fight alongside "soft-bellied river wolves."

Character tie: Skye, as a Silverrush guard, is sent to negotiate with Greyspire. Her mismatched eyes and blue ribbons make her an outsider among the scarred mountain wolves. She must prove her worth without revealing the old hunter's scars hidden beneath the ribbons.

*Mistmoor Subplot: "The Belly's Silence"*
The Belly-Rip has stopped chewing. Wolves who go to listen hear only their own heartbeats. The silence has weight, pressing on their skulls.


[6/12/2026 10:23 PM] lizardpelt
The swamp's healer Mirewort (a mute, rot-eaten wolf with missing toes) is lowered into the Rip on vines. He returns changed — not Drown-Sick, but empty. He no longer prays to the Maw. He just stares at the moon.

Conflict: The pack must decide: force-feed him to the Rip to wake it, or let the silence spread?

*Thistlehide Subplot: "The Memory That Bites Back"*
Kanami, the blind perpetual pup, begins naming wolves who have been forgotten — wolves erased from the pack's memory because no one howled for them. Their names cause physical pain to those who hear them. Wolves collapse, bleeding from the nose, as if the forgotten dead are pushing back into the world.

Finn'pelt must decide whether to believe the cursed pup or follow his enforcers who demand her death. His sister Mossgaze quietly gathers information, knowing that whoever moves against Kanami will have to go through the alpha first.

River'Shroud stands guard over the nursery dens, her antlers catching the moonlight, unable to speak but radiating threat to anyone who approaches the pups — including Pale'Step, who watches Kanami with a mix of jealousy (another cursed white pup) and reluctant sympathy.

Pale'Step, despite her sharp tongue and mean streak, begins leaving her dried asthma leaves near Kanami's hiding spots. She tells herself it's because she doesn't want the omen to die and curse the pack further. It is not that.

Conflict: A faction forms: those who believe the forgotten dead deserve to be remembered (even at the cost of pain) and those who believe the past should stay buried. Finn'pelt's rigid thinking struggles with supernatural events he cannot fight with his teeth.


[6/12/2026 10:27 PM] lizardpelt
*Silverrush Subplot: "The Warmth Below"*
Silverrush sends scouts upstream to investigate the warm water. They find a crack in the riverbed, a fissure that glows faintly orange. Heat shimmers above it. Something is burning underground.

Skye is part of the scout team. Her dire wolf size makes her a formidable fighter, but her tendency to get distracted when hunting nearly costs the team their lives when they stumble into Greyspire territory.

Conflict: A power struggle emerges in Silverrush. The alpha's authority is challenged by a wolf who claims she is hiding the truth about the warmth — that she knows the Maw is waking and is trying to flee instead of fight. Skye must choose between her oath to the alpha and her growing unease.

**The Midpoint (The Twist)**

A joint expedition, wolves from all four packs, including Finn'pelt, Mossgaze, River'Shroud (carrying Pale'Step on her back), Skye, and Mirewort, reaches the paper mill.

They find it abandoned. Not recently. Years ago. The machinery is rusted. The logging roads are overgrown. Human skeletons lie in the workers' barracks, some curled in sleep, some clawing at doors that were never locked.

The mill never reopened. The warmth in the river, the blood on the mountain, the silence of the Belly-Rip: none of it is the mill's doing.

In the mill's deepest basement, carved into the stone floor, is a massive tooth. A fossilized tooth, bigger than a wolf, bigger than a bear. It is chipped and cracked, as if something bit down on iron.

Kanami, carried in River'Shroud's massive jaws, lifts her blind face. She speaks in a voice that is not her own: low, wet, *old*.


[6/12/2026 10:28 PM] lizardpelt
"They found my tooth. They put it in the ground. They built their iron house on top of me. And I could not chew. I could not digest. I could only dream. I dreamed of wolves. I dreamed of the pack that was one. And now I am awake. And I am so, so hungry."

The sound, the wet click-click-hiss of Kanami's jaws, echoes off the walls. But now it is slower. Louder. It is the sound of the Maw chewing.

Pale'Step buries her face in River'Shroud's thick fur, her small body shaking with an asthma attack. The mute hunter cannot comfort her with words, but she presses her massive head against the pup's flank and does not move.

The wolves flee. The tooth remains.


[6/12/2026 10:30 PM] lizardpelt
**Bad Guys Close In**
The packs turn on each other.

Greyspire accuses Silverrush of leading them into a trap.

Mistmoor claims the Maw is their god.

Thistlehide protects Kanami, but she grows weaker — the Maw's voice is consuming her.

Silverrush fractures: the alpha is challenged. Skye stands with the alpha, but her loyalty is tested when she learns the challenger once saved her life as a pup.

The river grows hotter. Fish die by the thousands. The stench of rot fills the air — but it is not swamp-rot. It is digestion rot.

Wolves report tremors: the ground shaking, rhythmic. Chewing.

Finn'pelt's hyper-vigilance becomes psychosis. He stops sleeping entirely, patrolling the borders in endless circles. Mossgaze watches him deteriorate and breaks her own rule of silence, speaking to him alone in the pre-dawn mist. He does not respond. He only stares at the moon.

River'Shroud is ambushed by Greyspire wolves while hunting. She kills two of them with her bare strength — snapping a spine, crushing a skull — but takes a wound to her throat that nearly finishes what the childhood infection started. Pale'Step finds her bleeding in the thistles and, despite her wheezing lungs and weak heart, drags a healing herb to the mute hunter's mouth. River'Shroud survives. She touches her nose to Pale'Step's pink bows. That is her thank you.

Kanami begins to forget who she is. When Mossgaze brings her food, the blind pup asks, "Are you my mother?" Mossgaze, who has never had pups and never will, says yes. It is the first lie she has ever told her brother. She does not regret it.


[6/12/2026 10:32 PM] lizardpelt
**The "All Is Lost" Moment**
Kanami dies.

Not killed — emptied. The Maw's voice leaves her body in a single, wet gasp. Her sightless eye-sockets fill with blood. She speaks one last time, in her own small, tired voice:

"It's not waking up because we woke it. It's waking up because it's done digesting. The last time it ate — the Sundering — it swallowed something that didn't belong. Something made of iron. And now... now it needs to throw it up. And we are standing in its throat."

She goes still.

Without her voice, the packs have no interpreter. The tremors worsen. The river boils. The mountain weeps blood so thick that Greyspire's dens flood with red.

Silverrush's alpha loses the challenge. Skye is exiled with her, both of them swimming downstream into the unknown.

Thistlehide's enforcers move against Finn'pelt. They do not kill him — they cannot, he is too strong — but they isolate him, declaring him Maw-touched. Mossgaze is forbidden from seeing him.

River'Shroud takes Pale'Step and Kanami's body into the deep thistles. She buries the blind pup beneath a root system, the way Thistlehide honors the dead. Pale'Step does not cry. She pulls one of her pink bows from her fur and places it on the grave.

Mistmoor's alpha throws herself into the Belly-Rip. The chewing does not resume. She is simply gone.

Greyspire declares war on all packs. "If the Maw wants to vomit, we will give it something to choke on."

**ACT III: THE RESOLUTION (25%)**

**Dark Night of the Soul**
The exiles gather downstream.

Skye and the deposed Silverrush alpha float to a gravel bank, half-drowned. They are found by Mirewort — the Mistmoor healer who went silent after his trip into the Belly-Rip. He is not dead, not alive, but waking. His voice returns, hoarse and cracked:

"The Maw does not want a sacrifice. It wants a memory. The Sundering wasn't a war. It was a feeding. One wolf offered herself to end the fighting. She jumped into the Maw's mouth with a human iron blade in her teeth. The Maw swallowed her. But the iron cut its belly from the inside. It's been bleeding hunger ever since."

He names the wolf: Ash of the First Pack. No pack remembers her. She was forgotten.

Skye realizes: the Maw's waking is not a threat. It is a request. It wants to vomit up the iron. But it cannot do it alone.

Meanwhile, in Thistlehide, Mossgaze breaks Finn'pelt out of his isolation den. Her brother is thinner, his ash-black coat matted, his golden eyes hollow. She does not speak. She simply stands beside him, shoulder to shoulder, the way they used to stand before dawn.

River'Shroud and Pale'Step find them. The mute hunter lowers her antlered head to Finn'pelt,  a sign of submission, but also of solidarity. Pale'Step stomps her tiny paw and says, "You look like garbage, Alpha. Fix it."

Finn'pelt laughs. It is the first time any of them have heard him laugh. It sounds like rocks grinding together. It is beautiful.


[6/12/2026 10:34 PM] lizardpelt
**The Climax**

The exiles return to the paper mill — the place where the tooth was buried. They are pursued by wolves from all four packs, each believing the others are trying to claim the Maw's power for themselves.

At the mill, Skye faces the wolf who deposed her alpha. River'Shroud fights off Greyspire warriors, her antlers cracking against their skulls, Pale'Step tucked beneath her belly, wheezing but alive. Mossgaze uses her small size and terrain knowledge to flank the enemy, appearing and disappearing in the thistles like a ghost.

Finn'pelt stands at the center. He does not fight. He waits.

The ground shakes. The tooth in the basement glows red-hot.

Mirewort steps forward. He carries the rusted belt knife — the human tool given to him years ago. It is iron. It is the same kind of iron Ash carried into the Maw.

"We don't need to fight the Maw. We need to help it vomit. Ash's blade is still inside. If we pull it out —"

Kanami's body, carried by Mossgaze in a sling of woven vine, stirs. Her eye-sockets are empty, but her jaw moves. The click-click-hiss returns — slow, deliberate, kind.

"Yes," she whispers. "Pull it out. And I will remember her. I will remember Ash. I will speak her name every night until the moon falls. Give me that, and I will close my eye. I will sleep again. I will let you live."

The wolves realize: the Maw is not a monster. It is a wounded ancestor. It has been in pain for generations because no one remembered the wolf who tried to save them.

The Thistlehide enforcers refuse to listen. They attack Finn'pelt.

He does not move. He lets their teeth bounce off his oil-slicked, armor-like coat. He lets them exhaust themselves against his body. Then he speaks — low, gravelly, quiet:

"I am the wall. You cannot break me. But you can remember with me."

One by one, the enforcers stop fighting. They drop their heads.


[6/12/2026 10:35 PM] lizardpelt
River'Shroud lowers her massive body to the ground, her antlers scraping the stone floor. Pale'Step climbs onto her back and glares at the enemy wolves with her bright blue eyes, the pink bows in her fur catching the orange glow of the tooth. "She said remember," the pup squeaks. "So shut up and remember."

The Resolution
The wolves — from all four packs — work together. They dig into the basement. They find the fossilized tooth, and beneath it, wrapped in roots and stone, a human iron blade, rusted to almost nothing.

Mirewort picks it up with his teeth. It burns his mouth. His lips blister. His tongue blisters. He carries it to the river's edge and drops it into the boiling water.

The river screams. Steam rises. The water cools. The red fades to brown, then to clear.

The tooth in the mill cracks and crumbles to dust.

Kanami's body smiles — a grotesque, empty expression — and then relaxes. The click-click-hiss stops.

The moon, which had been growing larger for weeks, shrinks back to its normal size. It is still bitten — the crescent-shaped chunk remains missing — but it is no longer approaching.

Mirewort collapses. His mouth is ruined. He will never speak again. But he nods at Skye, and Skye understands.

She howls. Not a war howl. Not a mourning howl. A naming howl.

"Ash. Ash of the First Pack. She jumped into the Maw to end the Sundering. She carried iron in her teeth. She has been inside the Maw ever since. Remember her."


[6/12/2026 10:36 PM] lizardpelt
One by one, the wolves join. Greyspire's warriors. Mistmoor's Bog-dwellers. Thistlehide's foragers. Silverrush's swimmers. They howl the name Ash until the moon sets and the sun rises.

Finn'pelt howls loudest of all. His voice cracks. His stoic mask shatters. Mossgaze presses against his side, and for once, he does not push her away.

River'Shroud cannot howl. She presses her nose to the ground and scrapes the name Ash into the dirt with her claw. Pale'Step copies her, scratching tiny letters in the mud. "Ash," she whispers. "Stupid name. Stupid brave wolf."

Skye ties a new blue ribbon around her leg — the color of the river after it cleared. She will never remove it.

**The New Normal**

The packs do not reunite. The Sundering is too old, the wounds too deep. But they sign a new pact — the Pact of the Remembered — carved into the Sundering Stone.

Neutral ground is expanded. Any wolf may travel to the mill ruins without fear.

Kanami's body is buried at the river's source, near the Weeping Stones. Mossgaze tends her grave, leaving flowers for the pup she pretended to mother.

Pale'Step's asthma improves in the cleaner air. She still wears her pink bows. She still insults everyone. But now, when River'Shroud returns from patrol, the tiny pup runs to meet her: wheezing, stumbling, but running.

Finn'pelt remains alpha of Thistlehide. He is softer now: not weak, but present. He sleeps. Mossgaze watches over him during the night so he does not have to watch alone.

Skye returns to Silverrush as the alpha's most trusted guard. She still gets distracted during hunts. She still stares at the moon. But she no longer searches for a place to belong. She has found it.


[6/12/2026 10:37 PM] lizardpelt
The moon remains bitten. The Maw is not gone: just sleeping again. The iron is out of its belly, but the scar remains.

And somewhere, in the dark between stars, a blind pup with no eyes watches the four packs and smiles.

"Remember me," she whispers. "And I will remember you."
