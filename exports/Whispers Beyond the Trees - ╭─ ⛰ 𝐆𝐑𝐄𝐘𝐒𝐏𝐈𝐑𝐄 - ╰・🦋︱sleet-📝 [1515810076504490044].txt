# NPC

**Name:** Sleet  
**Pack:** Greyspire  
**Rank:** Diplomat  
**Age:** 6 years  
**Sexuality:** Heterosexual  
**Gender/Pronouns:** Female (she/her)

**Appearance:**  
Sleet is a lean, silver-white wolf with a coat that matches the winter mountains: pale gray, almost blue in shadow, with darker tips on her ears and tail. Her fur is thick and double-layered, making her look larger than she is. Her eyes are a cool, pale gray, and she has a small, neat scar on her upper lip (from a diplomatic mission gone wrong). She moves with deliberate, economical grace, never wasting a gesture. Her tail is carried level with her spine: a sign of confidence in Greyspire body language.

**Personality:**  

Sleet is Greyspire's voice to the outside world: a role that requires her to be both threatening and trustworthy. She has mastered the balance: she speaks softly, offers fair terms, and reminds the other packs that Greyspire's teeth are always in the background. Other diplomats respect her. They do not like her. That is fine with her.

She genuinely believes that Greyspire's harsh ways are necessary. The mountains do not forgive weakness. But she also believes that unnecessary bloodshed is foolish. She would rather negotiate a truce than fight a war: not out of kindness, but out of efficiency.

**Backstory:**  
Sleet was born to a Stoneguard father and a diplomat mother. Her mother taught her that words are weapons: sharper than teeth, longer-reaching than claws. Sleet learned to read wolves, to find their fears, to offer them just enough hope to keep them from attacking.

**RP Sample:**  
Sleet sat across from the Thistlehide diplomat, her silver-white fur blending with the snow behind her. The meeting was neutral ground; a frozen creek between territories. Neither pack had attacked yet. That was progress.

"Your alpha wants access to the mountain hunting grounds," Sleet said. Her voice was soft, almost friendly. "In exchange, you offer... what? Memories? Bark burials?"

The Thistlehide wolf bristled. "We offer peace."

"Peace is not a commodity. It is the absence of war. You cannot trade what you already have." Sleet tilted her head. "Try again, or I'll start thinking you're a *deerheart*."

The other diplomat was silent. Sleet waited. She could wait all day. She had waited through blizzards, through sieges, through the slow death of hope. A few more moments of *sharing tongues*? Nothing.

**Other:** Open to plots where she must negotiate with hostile packs, or where her loyalty is tested by a better offer.
