# NPC

**Name:** Driftpup  
**Pack:** Silverrush  
**Rank:** Pup (Driftwood-born — outsider parents)  
**Age:** 3 moons  
**Sexuality:** Too young  
**Gender/Pronouns:** Male (he/him)

**Appearance:**  
Driftpup is a small, scruffy pup with a coat of dusty gray and brown — like driftwood left too long in the sun. His fur is patchy and thin, and he has a pale white blaze on his forehead. His eyes are a bright, nervous hazel, and they dart constantly. His tail is short and stubby (a birth defect). His paws are too large for his body, making him trip often. He smells faintly of smoke — a remnant of the fire that killed his birth pack.

**Personality:**  
*Anxious, quiet, grateful, easily startled, desperately eager to belong.*  

Driftpup was not born in Silverrush. He was found on the riverbank, alone, after a wildfire destroyed his birth pack. He does not talk about it. He does not talk much at all. He follows the older Driftwood wolves around, watching them carry stones, trying to imitate them. He wants to be useful. He is terrified of being thrown out.

He flinches at loud noises — a cracking stick, a raised voice, the rumble of thunder. He sleeps curled in a tight ball, hidden under ferns. But he is gentle. He shares his food. He grooms the other pups. He wants to be good.

**Backstory:**  
Driftpup’s birth pack lived far to the south, in a dry forest of pine and scrub. They were not part of the four packs: they were loners, rogues, outcasts. A wildfire — caused by twoleg carelessness — swept through their territory when Driftpup was one moon old. He was the only survivor. He wandered for a quarter-moon, eating insects and drinking from puddles, until he collapsed on Silverrush’s riverbank.

Ripple found him. She brought him to the healer. Ashpool allowed him to stay, but he is not truly Silverrush-born — he is Driftwood, an outsider, always proving himself.

**RP Sample:**  
Driftpup sat at the edge of the river, his scruffy gray coat blending with the driftwood. A group of Silverrush-born pups were playing in the shallows, splashing and chasing. He wanted to join them. He did not dare.

He picked up a small stone and carried it to the pile on the far bank. Then another. Then another. That was what Driftwood wolves did. They carried stones. They proved themselves.

A shadow fell over him. He flinched.

Ripplepup stood there, dripping wet. “Why aren’t you playing?”

He did not answer. He did not know how to explain.

Ripplepup grabbed a stone and carried it to the pile. Then she came back and did it again. She did not say anything. She just carried stones beside him.

Driftpup’s eyes burned. He blinked and kept carrying.
