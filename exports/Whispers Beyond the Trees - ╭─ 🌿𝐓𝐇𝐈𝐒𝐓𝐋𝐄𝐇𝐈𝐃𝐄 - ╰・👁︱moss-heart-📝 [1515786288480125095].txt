# NPC

**Name:** Mossheart  
**Pack:** Thistlehide  
**Rank:** Scout  
**Age:** 3 years  
**Sexuality:** Homosexual  
**Gender/Pronouns:** Male (he/him)

**Appearance:**  
Mossheart is a lean, wiry wolf with a golden-brown coat that darkens to umber along his spine. His fur is thick and bristly, like the thistle plant he is named for, and it often has burrs and twigs tangled in it. His eyes are a sharp, intelligent green, and his ears are large and constantly moving, tracking sounds. He has a thin white scar on his cheek from a thorn bush that nearly took his eye.

**Personality:**  

Mossheart is the pack’s best scout: not because he is brave, but because he is *nervous*. He is always looking for danger, always listening for threats, always planning an escape route. His anxiety makes him excellent at his job. It also makes him exhausting to be around.

He keeps everything in order: his den, his herb pouch, his patrol routes. He cannot stand mess or unpredictability. The recent events, the blind pup, the mill, the Maw, have pushed him to the edge of his sanity. He wants things to go back to normal. He knows they never will.

**Backstory:**  
Mossheart was born to a scout mother and a hunter father. Both are still alive, a rarity, and both are deeply disappointed in him. He is not strong enough to be a hunter. He is not brave enough to be a guard. He is *only* a scout, running away from danger instead of toward it.

He has never told anyone that he is in love with a Silverrush wolf: a hunter he met at the border three moons ago. They meet in secret, exchange news, share meals. Mossheart knows it is forbidden. He does not care. It is the only warmth in his life.

**Skills:**
- **Stealth (Dexterity +4):** Moves through thistle and bramble without a sound.
- **Tracking (Intelligence +3):** Can read the forest like a map.
- **Perception (Wisdom +3):** Notices tiny changes — a snapped twig, a shifted stone.

**RP Sample:**  
Mossheart pressed his belly to the ground, his bristly fur blending with the thistle patch. Below him, in the clearing, a Silverrush wolf sat alone. His heart raced.

He crept forward, one paw at a time. The Silverrush wolf turned. Green eyes met green eyes.

“You’re late,” the wolf said.

“I know.” Mossheart slipped into the clearing, his fur still bristling with anxiety. “There was a patrol. I had to hide.”

The Silverrush wolf, a grey male named Rivenmaw, nuzzled his cheek. “You’re always hiding.”

“I’m a scout. It’s what I do.”

They sat together, close, watching the sun set through the trees. Mossheart wanted to say something: something about the missing moon, about the blind pup, about the fear that gnawed at his stomach. But he did not. He just leaned into his warmth and pretended, for a moment, that the world was not ending.

**Other:** Open to plots where his secret romance is discovered, or where his anxiety gets someone killed.
