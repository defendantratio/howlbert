# NPC

**Name:** Murkvein  
**Pack:** Mistmoor  
**Rank:** Alpha (Rotmother)  
**Age:** 9 years  
**Sexuality:** Heterosexual  
**Gender/Pronouns:** Female (she/her)

**Appearance:**  
Murkvein is a gaunt, long-limbed wolf with a coat the color of stagnant water: greenish-gray, streaked with darker patches that look like oil slicks. Her fur is sparse and greasy, and her skin shows through in places, giving her a skeletal appearance. Her eyes are a pale, luminous green, and she has no tail; lost to rot-lung necrosis when she was young. She moves with a sinuous, boneless grace that unsettles other wolves. Her teeth are yellowed and worn, but her bite is *fast*.

**Personality:**  

Murkvein has ruled Mistmoor for five years, longer than any Rotmother in recent memory. She did it by being *patient* — waiting for rivals to make mistakes, then drowning them in the Belly-Rip. She believes the Maw speaks directly to her through dreams, and she has the scars to prove it: ritual cuts on her belly, each one representing a prophecy she has received.

She is not insane, or if she is, her insanity is *useful*. She correctly predicted the red river, the missing moon chunk, and the birth of the blind pup in Thistlehide. Her wolves fear her, but they also *trust* her. She has never been wrong.


[6/14/2026 1:58 PM] lizardpelt
**Backstory:**  
Murkvein was born to a previous Rotmother, but her mother died before she could name an heir. The pack tore itself apart in succession wars. Murkvein survived by hiding in the Belly-Rip’s edge, eating fungus and drinking the warm, salt-tinged water. When she emerged a year later, she was *changed*: her tail gone, her eyes green, her voice a whisper that carried across the swamp. She challenged the current Rotmother and won, not through strength but through *certainty*. She told the pack the Maw had chosen her. They believed.

She has kept the pack together through famine, Twoleg encroachment, and the recent silence of the Belly-Rip. She is old now, tired, but she will not step down. She is waiting for one more prophecy; the one that tells her how to die.

**Skills:**

- **Persuasion (Charisma +2):** Her whisper can convince wolves to do almost anything. Her voice is honeyed and soft, a strange thing in a swamp of rot and ruin. Wolves find themselves agreeing before they realize what they've promised.
- **Poison & Cure Lore (Wisdom +4):** Knows every poison and cure in the swamp, from the common rot-lichen to the rare black-vein mushroom that grows only in the Belly-Rip's shadow. Her knowledge rivals even the most dedicated healers.
- **Intimidation (Charisma +3):** Her green eyes and boneless movements terrify even her own pack. When she stares, wolves feel the Maw staring back. Few meet her gaze willingly.

**Weaknesses:**

- **Old Age (Strength -4):** Her body is failing. Her joints ache constantly, her lungs rattle with the swamp's kiss, and she tires after a short sprint. She cannot fight for long, and a single hard fall could break her brittle bones.
- **No Tail (Dexterity -2):** She lost her tail to a gator's jaws in her youth. Now she struggles to balance in water, which is deadly in Mistmoor's treacherous shallows. She avoids swimming whenever possible and clings to roots when forced.
- **Paranoid about Mirewort (Wisdom -1):** She suspects the healer has touched the Maw in ways she cannot control. Her distrust colors every interaction with him: she questions his cures, refuses his aid, and watches him like a hawk watches a snake. This paranoia has cost her health more than once.

**RP Sample:**  
Murkvein sat at the edge of the Belly-Rip, her green eyes reflecting the dark water. The chewing had resumed — slower than before, but *present*. She reached out a claw and touched the sinkhole’s lip.

“You spoke to him,” she murmured. “Mirewort. You told him things you never told me.”

The water bubbled. She took it as a reply.

“I am not jealous,” she lied. “I am *curious*. Why him? Why not your faithful daughter?”

No answer. The chewing continued, indifferent.

Murkvein stood, her gaunt frame swaying. “Fine. Keep your secrets. I have my own.”

She turned and limped back toward the dens, her missing tail leaving a strange, unbalanced trail in the mud. She stepped over a carrion of a water-rat, too rotten even for the scavengers.

**Other:** Open to plots where she is challenged for leadership, or where her final prophecy is revealed.
