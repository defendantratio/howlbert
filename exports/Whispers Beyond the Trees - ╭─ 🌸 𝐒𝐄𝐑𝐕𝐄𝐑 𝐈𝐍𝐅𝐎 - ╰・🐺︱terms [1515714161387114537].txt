## Basic Terms

**Carrion**  
> A dead carcass that has begun to rot. Also used as an insult for a wolf with no strength or honor.

**Scat**  
> Feces.

**Fresh-kill**  
> A recently killed prey animal brought back to the pack to eat. Fresh-kill is hunted by hunters and yearlings, then placed in the fresh-kill cache. Elders, pups, den-keepers, and sick wolves eat first, followed by hunters and yearlings. Leftovers are buried or left for scavengers.

**Hearth-hound**  
> A domesticated pet dog that lives with Twolegs. Often viewed as soft, foolish, and overly dependent by wild wolves.

**Loner**  
> A wolf that lives apart from any pack and does not defend a fixed territory.

**Marking scat**  
> Defecating, especially to scent-mark territory boundaries.

**Monster**  
> A car or another similar vehicle. Often described when wolves are near Twolegs or the Thunderpath.

**Rend**  
> A violent fight between wolves, often over a kill or boundary dispute. 

**Rogue**  
> A hostile wolf without a permanent home, who roams across pack lands and respects no boundaries. Often violent.

**Sharing tongues**  
> Wolves grooming each other while sharing the latest gossip. A wolf lies on the ground, talking, while the other grooms their fur and listens.

**She-wolf / Pup**  
> A female wolf / A wolf cub.

**Sun-sink-place**  
> An ocean to the west of the packs' territories, where the sun appears to sink into the water.

**Tree-gulper**  
> A bulldozer—a roaring monster that devours trees and tears up the earth.

**Thunderpath**  
> A paved road with an extremely acrid smell that monsters (cars) often go across.

**He-wolf / Pup**  
> A male wolf.

## Seasons & Time

**Newgrowth**  
> The season of spring, when plants begin to sprout and prey returns.

**Highsun**  
> The season of summer, when days are long and prey is plentiful.

**Leaf-drop**  
> The season of autumn, when leaves fall and temperatures cool.

**Leaf-bare**  
> The season of winter, when snow covers the ground and prey is scarce.

**Moon**  
> The time between one full moon and the next, about thirty days.

**Moonhigh**  
> When the moon is highest in the sky, around midnight.

**Moonrise**  
> When the moon rises above the horizon.

**Half-moon**  
> Two weeks, half a month.

**Quarter-moon**  
> One week.

**Sunhigh**  
> Noon, when the sun is at its peak.

**Sunrise**  
> One day in wolf time (e.g., "Two sunrises ago").

**Season**  
> One quarter of a year. Four seasons make one full cycle of the year.


## Measurements

**Wolf-claw length**  
> About the length of a wolf's claw; roughly 1.5 inches (3.8 cm).

**Deer-length**  
> About the length of a deer from nose to tail; approximately 6–8 feet (1.8–2.4 m).

**Frog-length**  
> About the length of a frog; roughly 2–4 inches (5–10 cm).

**Pupstep**  
> About the length of a wolf pup's step; roughly 2–3 inches (5–7.5 cm).

**Hare-hop**  
> About the distance of a hare's jump; roughly 18–24 inches (45–60 cm).

**Reed-length**  
> About the length of a tall reed; up to 5 feet (1.5 m).

**Vole-length**  
> About the length of a vole; roughly 3–5 inches (7.5–12.5 cm).

**Snout-length**  
> About the length of a wolf's muzzle; roughly 4–6 inches (10–15 cm).

**Pawstep**  
> About the length of a grown wolf's step; roughly 12–18 inches (30–45 cm).

**Tail-length**  
> About the length of a wolf's tail; roughly 15–20 inches (38–50 cm).

**Tree-length**  
> About the length of a mature tree; roughly 40–60 feet (12–18 m).

## Insults

**Deerheart**  
> A cowardly wolf, one who flees like a startled deer instead of standing with the pack.

**Carrion-breath**  
> A wolf whose breath stinks of rotten meat; implies laziness or eating spoiled kills.

**Frog-gut**  
> A weak, pathetic wolf with no stamina. Frogs are small, slimy prey barely worth a wolf’s time.

**Vole-snout**  
> A wolf who noses around in unimportant matters; a gossip or meddler.

**Pup-brain**  
> A foolish, naive wolf who acts like a helpless pup instead of an adult.

**Coyote**  
> A traitorous or untrustworthy wolf. Coyotes are rivals and scavengers, despised by wolves.

**Scat-licker**  
> A disgusting, lowly wolf with no pride or cleanliness.

**Mud-paw**  
> A clumsy wolf who can’t hunt silently or keep a clean pelt.

**Hollow-fang**  
> A wolf who talks big but can’t back it up in a hunt or fight.

**Snake**  
> A sneaky, deceitful wolf who twists words and avoids direct confrontation.

**Oak-head**  
> A stupid, dense wolf who doesn’t learn from experience.
