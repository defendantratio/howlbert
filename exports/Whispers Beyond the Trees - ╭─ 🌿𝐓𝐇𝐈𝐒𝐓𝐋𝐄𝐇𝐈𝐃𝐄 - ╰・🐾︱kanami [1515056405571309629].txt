**Appearance:**

Kanami is a delicate, snow-white wolf who suffers from a rare birth defect that completely halted her physical growth, leaving her permanently trapped in the small, fragile body of a six-month-old pup. Her pristine white coat acts as a beacon in the dark Thistlehide woods, contrasting sharply with her glassy, milky-white eyes that have been completely blind since birth. A dark, soot-colored band wraps tightly around her lower left back leg — a marking that superstitious pack members whisper is the literal touch of a curse.

**Personality:**

- **Hyper-vigilant:** Forced to stay constantly alert, using her ears to detect incoming threats or hostile packmates.  
- **Stubbornly independent:** Refuses to rely on others, pushing her tiny body to its absolute limits to prove she isn't dead weight.  
- **Quiet and guarded:** Rarely speaks or makes noise, knowing that drawing attention to her cursed status invites cruelty.  
- **Omen-burdened:** Carries a heavy, silent sadness, half-believing the pack's whispers that her very existence brings bad luck.  
- **Secretly fearless:** Because she has lived on the brink of execution her whole life, she does not fear the dangerous areas of the woods.

Kanami is an island of quiet resilience. Because she cannot grow and cannot see, she has developed an uncanny, ghostly way of moving through the territory, fading into the brush like a phantom. She is acutely aware of the hatred directed toward her; she hears the dark mutterings of the elders and the way mothers pull their healthy pups away when she passes. This has made her incredibly insular and guarded, but it has also forged an iron-willed survival instinct. She does not beg for pity, choosing instead to let her absolute silence be her shield.


[6/14/2026 6:14 PM] lizardpelt
**Backstory:**

Kanami’s birth was met with immediate horror by the Thistlehide pack. Born completely blind and bearing an eerie, unblemished white coat with a stark black leg band, the elders immediately declared her a walking bad omen — a curse sent by the Maw to bring rot to the pack's bloodline. Believing her presence would bring bad luck to the seasonal hunts, her parents abandoned her to the elements on the very day she opened her sightless eyes.

Her survival is viewed by the pack not as a miracle, but as dark magic. Against all odds, she survived by hiding in root systems and hunting tiny creatures solely by sound. As the moons pressed on, a secondary defect became undeniably clear: Kanami stopped growing entirely. While her littermates grew into large, powerful hunters, Kanami remained tiny, a perpetual pup frozen in time. Whenever a hunting party failed or a border skirmish went poorly, the pack blamed the "White Omen" hiding in their shadows.

When Finn’pelt eventually discovered her living on the fringes, his strict but protective nature created a massive rift in the pack. While the superstitious elders and guards wanted her executed to cleanse the bad luck, Finn’pelt refused to kill a pack-born wolf who had proven she could feed herself. He allowed her to stay on probation. Kanami now walks a razor's edge, knowing that one major pack disaster could cause the superstitious wolves to force her into a living bark-burial to lift her "curse."

**Breakdown:**

**Maw-Belief:**

Heretic — The pack says the Maw cursed her. In return, she quietly hates the forest roots and the bark-burials, believing the entity they worship is nothing but a cruel monster. She does not speak this aloud — that would be a death sentence — but in her heart, she has no faith, only resentment and cold survival.

**RP Sample:**

The world was a complex, swirling tapestry of snaps, clicks, and rushing air. Kanami sat completely motionless beneath the low-hanging ferns, her snow-white fur blending poorly with the dark loam, but her stillness keeping her perfectly hidden. Her oversized ears twitched independently, filtering out the distant, gravelly howl of the alpha on the ridge and focusing entirely on a rhythmic, heavy stepping sound thirty paces ahead. One, two… pause. A heavy hoof striking wet slate. A deer.

She lowered her belly to the ground, her paws pressing softly into the cold moss. She could not see the curve of the animal's flank or the color of its coat, but she knew exactly how tall it was by the angle the sound traveled to her ears. She slipped forward, shifting her weight methodically to ensure not a single dry twig snapped beneath her black-banded hind leg.
