# NPC

**Name:** Ashbark  
**Pack:** Thistlehide  
**Rank:** Hunter  
**Age:** 5 years  
**Sexuality:** Heterosexual  
**Gender/Pronouns:** Male (he/him)

**Appearance:**  
Ashbark is a lean, dark-furred wolf with a coat of charcoal gray and soot-black,  the color of burned wood. His fur is thick and bristly, often tangled with ash from the old burn scars in Thistlehide’s southern edge. His eyes are a flat, pale brown, and he has a deep scratch across his muzzle from a hunting accident years ago. His ears are heavily notched, and his tail is thin and whip-like. He moves with a tense, hunched posture, as if expecting a blow.

**Personality:**  
*Guilty, avoidant, quiet, superstitious, secretly ashamed.*  

Ashbark does not talk about Kanami. When other wolves mention the White Omen, he lowers his head and walks away. He was the one who carried her to the thistles and left her there. He tells himself it was necessary: the elders said she was cursed, that she would bring rot to the pack. But he dreams about her every night. In the dreams, she is not blind. She stares at him with those milky eyes and asks, *“Why?”*

He has never answered. He does not know how.

**Backstory:**  
Ashbark was born in Thistlehide to a long line of hunters. He was never exceptional, but he was reliable: he brought back fresh-kill, guarded the borders, followed the alpha’s orders. When Kanami was born blind and marked with the black leg band, the elders declared her a curse. Ashbark did not argue. He took her to the thistles and left her.

He has regretted it every day since. When Finn’pelt allowed her to stay on probation, Ashbark felt both relief and terror. He avoids her. He cannot look at her. He knows she knows who he is.

Ashbark stood at the edge of the hunting party, his dark coat blending with the charred trees. He should have been watching for prey. Instead, he was watching Kanami. She sat alone near the healer’s den, her white fur glowing in the dusk light.

She turned her blind face toward him. His heart stopped.

“Ashbark.” The alpha’s voice. He flinched. “The deer. South ridge.”

He nodded and fled into the trees, not looking back.
