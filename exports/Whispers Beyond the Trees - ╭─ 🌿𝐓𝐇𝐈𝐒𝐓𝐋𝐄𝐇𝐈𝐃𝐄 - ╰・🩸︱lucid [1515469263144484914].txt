**Name:** Lucid (often called Lucivor by elders who remember his full naming)  
**Pack:** Thistlehide  
**Rank:** Hunter  
**Age:** 5 years  
**Sexuality:** Straight  
**Gender/Pronouns:** Male (he/him)

**Appearance:**

Lucid is a dark brown wolf with a coat that blends into the shadowed undergrowth of Thistlehide’s deepest woods. His fur is thick and coarse, often matted with burrs and dried mud. His eyes are amber — the left one marred by a milky haze, the pupil misshapen from a twoleg beating when he was a pup. The damaged eye still tracks movement but cannot see detail. A faded band of scarred, hairless skin wraps around his right foreleg, a wound from the same humans that never fully healed. He keeps the leg wrapped in a strip of chewed bark, not because it still bleeds, but because the pressure eases the old ache.

**Personality:**

Helpful, self-sacrificing, anxious about failure, protective, quietly stubborn.

Lucid will try anything to help anyone, no matter the consequence. He is the first to volunteer for a dangerous patrol, the last to eat from the fresh-kill pile, and the wolf who sits with injured packmates long after the healer has left. He is terrified of failing others: of being too slow, too weak, too blind to save someone who needed him.

He is not the loudest hunter, nor the most skilled. But he is *reliable*. When Lucid says he will guard a den, he does not sleep until relieved. When he promises to track a lost pup, he follows the trail until his paws bleed. His packmates sometimes take advantage of his generosity. He does not seem to notice  or if he does, he does not care.

**Backstory:**

Lucid was born in the darkened woods south of Thistlehide territory, not to wolves but to Twolegs. He was a hearth-hound: a domesticated pup taken from his mother too early, kept on a rope, fed scraps, and beaten when he growled. The twolegs called him vicious because he bit back. They broke his leg with a iron pipe and left him in a ditch to die.

He did not die. A Thistlehide patrol found him three days later, half-starved, his leg swollen and black. The healer at the time — an old Fur-Giver (healer) named Thornroot — wanted to leave him. A hearth-hound was not a wolf. But the alpha overruled, and Lucid was brought into the pack.

It took him a full season to learn to walk without a limp. It took him another to learn to hunt: his blind eye ruined his depth perception, and his old leg ached in the cold. But he persisted. He learned to ambush from the blind side, to use his nose instead of his eyes, to drive prey toward younger, faster hunters. He became useful.

He has never seen a twoleg since his rescue. He dreams about them often: the smell of iron and beer, the crack of the pipe, the laughter. He wakes grinding his teeth and does not tell anyone why.

**Skills:**
- **Hunting (Dexterity +4):** Specializes in driving prey toward ambush points. Uses his damaged eye to his advantage — prey underestimates him.
- **Tracking (Intelligence +3):** Excellent nose. Can follow a cold trail for a quarter-moon.
- **Stealth (Dexterity +3):** Moves quietly through dry leaves and brittle undergrowth. Learned to be silent as a hearth-hound sneaking scraps.

- **Old Leg Injury (Dexterity -3):** The shattered bone healed poorly. He cannot sprint for long distances and limps in cold weather.
- **Damaged Eye (Wisdom -2):** Poor depth perception. Struggles to judge distance, especially in low light.
- **Self-Sacrificing to a Fault (Charisma -2):** Will give up his own share of food, his own place in the den, his own safety even when it is not needed. Packmates sometimes exploit this.

**Breakdown:**

- **Strength (6):** Above average. His hearth-hound lineage gave him a compact, muscular build.
- **Dexterity (5):** Average speed, but his stealth training compensates.
- **Survival (4):** Below average. He still struggles to identify safe mushrooms and often relies on other foragers.
- **Intelligence (1):** He is not clever. He does not strategize. He follows orders and trusts his instincts.
- **Charisma (1):** Awkward, quiet, and scarred. Other wolves find him hard to approach.
- **Wisdom (1):** Poor judgment. He trusts too easily and often misreads packmates' intentions.


**Maw-Belief:**

Doubter — Lucid was not raised with the Maw’s stories. He learned them as an adult, and they felt like fairy tales told to pups. He respects the pack’s rituals because they matter to his packmates, but he does not pray. He cannot believe in a god that would let a pup be beaten by twolegs and left in a ditch.

Lucid limped toward the center of the clearing, his dark brown coat blending with the evening shadows. The pack was gathered — hunters, foragers, the alpha. He dipped his head to Finn’pelt.

“The pups are hungry,” he said, his voice low and rough. “We should hunt now, before moonhigh.”


[6/14/2026 5:57 PM] lizardpelt
Finn’pelt’s golden eyes studied him. Lucid did not flinch. He was used to being studied.

“Take two hunters,” the alpha said. “Bring back fresh-kill within a quarter-moon.”

Lucid nodded and turned. He did not ask which hunters. He would find them. He always did.

As he padded toward the dens, he passed Mossgaze, who was sorting roots near the healer’s den. She looked up, startled, and dropped a bundle of feverfew.

Lucid stopped. He bent, picked up the herbs with his teeth, and placed them gently at her paws. Then he continued on, not waiting for thanks.

He never waited for thanks.
