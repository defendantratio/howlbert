# 1. CORE MECHANICS

### Rolling
Roll **1d20** and add the relevant attribute modifier, plus a **out of 5 skill bonus** if your character is proficient in a relevant skill.

- Total ≥ DC → Success
- Total < DC → Failure
- Natural 1 → Critical failure (something goes badly wrong)
- Natural 20 → Critical success (exceptional outcome or lasting boon)

### Action Difficulty
- **Simple** (eating, walking, smelling) — no roll
- **Routine** (tracking prey, climbing a familiar slope) — DC 10 (Easy)
- **Challenging** (fighting an enemy wolf, foraging in a blizzard) — DC 15 (Moderate)
- **Desperate** (jumping a chasm, treating a fatal wound with few herbs) — DC 20 (Hard)
- **Legendary** (curing a spirit-curse with wolfsbane, surviving the Whisper Den) — DC 25 (Nearly impossible)

### Quick Attribute Modifier Reference
- Score 1 → –3
- Score 2 → –2
- Score 3 → –2
- Score 4 → –1
- Score 5 → 0
- Score 6 → +1
- Score 7 → +1
- Score 8 → +2
- Score 9 → +2
- Score 10 → +3

## 2. ATTRIBUTES

Every wolf has six attributes, each rated 1–10:

- **Strength** – Biting, holding, carrying, breaking, grappling.
- **Dexterity** – Stealth, climbing, dodging, swimming, speed.
- **Survival/Constitution** – Foraging, enduring harsh conditions, stamina, resisting poison/disease, navigation, fertility.
- **Intelligence** – Herb knowledge, memory, plant identification, tracking logic.
- **Charisma** – Howling, intimidation, persuasion, pack influence, courtship.
- **Wisdom** – Perception, diagnosis, insight, resisting fear/illusion, pheromone detection, and willpower.

## 5. COMBAT

**Initiative**: Roll 1d20 + Dexterity modifier. Highest total acts first.

**Turn Structure**: Move + one Action (bite, claw, use herb/item, etc.)

**Bite**: Attacker rolls 1d20 + Strength modifier (+ Hunting if proficient) vs. defender's 1d20 + Dexterity modifier. Hit: 1d6 + Strength modifier damage.

**Claw**: Attacker rolls 1d20 + Dexterity modifier vs. defender's 1d20 + Dexterity modifier. Hit: 1d4 + Dexterity modifier damage.

**Grapple**: Opposed Strength vs. Strength roll. A successful pin reduces the target's available actions.

**Sprint**: Use your action to move double your normal speed. Does not allow attacks during that turn.

### Critical Hits & Fumbles 

**Natural 20 on attack (Critical hit):** Roll 1d4 for extra effect:
- 1: Bonus damage (+1d4)
- 2: Knock target prone
- 3: Disarm (target drops held item or loses grip)
- 4: Temporary bleed (target loses 1 HP per round for 3 rounds)

**Natural 1 on attack (Critical fumble):** Roll 1d4:
- 1: Drop guard – enemy gets a free attack on you
- 2: Strain muscle – disadvantage on your next attack roll
- 3: Stumble – you fall prone
- 4: Bite own tongue – 1 damage and cannot speak/use vocal skills for 1 round

**Health (HP)**: HP = 10 + Strength modifier + Survival/Constitution modifier.  
*Example: Strength +2, Survival/Constitution +1 → HP = 13.*

**Healing**: Without herbs, recover 1 HP per long rest. With proper herbs (e.g. comfrey poultice), recover 1d4+1 HP per short rest, up to 3 times per day.

**Short rest**: 10–30 minutes of light activity (eating, grooming).  
**Long rest**: 6–8 hours of sleep.

## 6. INJURY, DEATH, INFECTION & EXHAUSTION

### Injury Table (1d10)
Roll 1d10 whenever a wolf drops to 0 HP or takes a critical hit:

1. **Broken tooth** — Disadvantage on bite attacks. No cure — permanent.
2. **Torn claw** — −1 to claw damage. Heals in 1 week, or 3 days with horsetail poultice.
3. **Deep gash** — Bleeding: lose 1 HP/hour until bandaged. Yarrow + cobwebs stops bleeding.
4. **Sprained leg** — Movement halved. Comfrey + 3 days rest.
5. **Fractured rib** — Disadvantage on Strength checks; cannot hunt. Comfrey poultice + 2 weeks rest.
6. **Concussion** — Disadvantage on Intelligence checks; memory loss. Dried skullcap + 1 week sleep.
7. **Punctured paw** — Disadvantage on Dexterity checks involving running or climbing. Heals in 1 week; binding with oak bark halves time.
8. **Infected wound** — Roll 1d20 + Survival/Constitution each morning. On a failure, lose 1 HP and gain 1 exhaustion level until treated with yarrow or goldenrod poultice.
9. **Torn ear / lost eye** — Permanent −1 to Perception (Wisdom) checks. No cure if eye is lost; torn ear heals cosmetically but penalty remains.
10. **Broken jaw** — Cannot bite or eat solid food. Requires liquid diet (broth, milk) and 3 weeks rest. Comfrey poultice reduces to 2 weeks.

### Infection
Wounds that are not cleaned within 24 hours have a chance to become infected. The GM may call for a Survival/Constitution save (DC 12) after a serious wound. Failure means the wound becomes **infected** (see injury #8 above).

- **Treatment:** Yarrow poultice or goldenrod poultice applied within 1 day cures infection without a roll. After 1 day, requires a Medicine check (DC 15).
- **Untreated infection:** Each day, roll Survival/Constitution DC 12. Failure: lose 1 HP and gain 1 exhaustion level. After 3 failures, the infection becomes gangrene (requires amputation or death).

### Long-Term Injuries
Some injuries leave permanent marks, even after healing. These are roleplay opportunities but may have minor mechanical effects.

- **Limp:** After a badly healed leg injury, movement speed reduced by 1/4 (round down).
- **Scarring:** No mechanical effect, but gives +1 to Intimidation checks against wolves who see the scar.
- **Chronic pain:** On cold or rainy days, disadvantage on the first Dexterity or Strength check of the day.
- **Fear of [trigger]:** After a traumatic injury (e.g., from a bear), the wolf may develop a fear. When facing that trigger, make a Wisdom save DC 12 or be frightened for 1 round.

Long-term injuries can be healed with herbs (wolfsbane, swamp milkweed) at the GM's discretion, or with months of rest.

### Death
Death is permanent. No magic brings wolves back.

A wolf at 0 HP is **dying** — they must make a Survival/Constitution save:
- Round 1: DC 10
- Round 2: DC 12
- Round 3: DC 15

If all three succeed, stabilize at 1 HP. If any fail, the wolf dies. Allies can attempt to stabilize with a **Medicine check (Wisdom)**. The DC is 15. If the helper has Herblore proficiency, they add the +2 bonus.  
**Yarrow** grants +2 to this check. **Cobwebs** stabilize automatically (no roll required).

**Mercy killing**: A dying wolf may request deathberries. This is a roleplay moment, not a dice roll.

### Exhaustion Levels
1. Disadvantage on skill checks.
2. Speed halved.
3. Disadvantage on attack rolls.
4. HP maximum halved.
5. Speed 0 (cannot move).
6. Death.

**Recovery**: One full day of rest (warm den, food, water) removes 1 exhaustion level.

## 7. SWIMMING & DROWNING

### Swimming
- **Swim speed** = half your normal movement (round down). Example: normal speed 6 → swim speed 3.
- **Calm water** (pond, slow river): No roll needed to stay afloat.
- **Rough water** (fast river, lake with waves): Roll Survival/Constitution DC 12 each minute (or each round in combat). Failure means you sink 1 foot and must spend your next action to surface; critical failure = start drowning.

### Holding Breath
- A wolf can hold its breath for **Survival/Constitution modifier + 3** rounds (minimum 1 round).
- After that, each round make a Survival/Constitution save DC 15. Failure = unconscious. Next round = death unless rescued.

### Drowning Rescue
- A drowning wolf can be pulled to the surface by another wolf making a Strength check DC 12 (grapple then swim up).
- Once at the surface, the drowning wolf automatically regains consciousness if rescued within 1 round.

## 8. FEAR OF FIRE

Wolves naturally fear open flame. This affects campfires, wildfires, torches carried by humans, etc.

### When Fire is Present
- Within 10 feet of open flame, make a **Wisdom save DC 12**.
- **Success:** No effect. You can act normally but remain wary.
- **Failure:** You gain the **frightened** condition:
  - Cannot willingly move closer to the fire.
  - Disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks while the fire is within line of sight.
- The effect lasts until you move more than 30 feet away from the fire **or** the fire is extinguished.

### Overcoming Fear
- An Alpha or packmate can howl or bark encouragement: **Charisma + Persuasion DC 14** as an action. Success removes the frightened condition for one ally within earshot.
- A wolf with the **Guard** role may reroll a failed Wisdom save against fire fear once per session.
- Using fire as a tool to intimidate: A wolf holding their ground against fire (e.g., defending a den from humans with torches) can roll **Charisma + Intimidation DC 15** to ignore fear for 1 round.

### Wildfire
- Wildfire (forest fire) causes fear at double range (20 feet). Additionally, each round near wildfire requires Survival/Constitution DC 15 or take 1d4 heat damage (smoke inhalation).

## 9. DISEASE MECHANICS

Several herbs in the compendium cure diseases (whitecough, blackcough, etc.). Use these rules for disease progression.

### Disease Stages
- **Mild (Whitecough):** Coughing, mild weakness. Disadvantage on Dexterity checks.
- **Severe (Blackcough):** Fever, wheezing. Disadvantage on Dexterity and Strength checks. Speed reduced by 1/4.
- **Deadly (Redcough):** Blood from mouth, extreme fatigue. Disadvantage on all physical checks, lose 1 HP per hour. Death in 1d4 days without treatment.

### Progression
Each day without treatment, roll **Survival/Constitution**:
- Mild: DC 12. Failure → progresses to Severe.
- Severe: DC 15. Failure → progresses to Deadly.
- Deadly: DC 18. Failure → take 1d4 damage + 1 exhaustion level. Death when HP reaches 0 or exhaustion level 6.

### Cures
- **Mild:** Coltsfoot, chickweed, or juniper berry tonic (1 dose ends disease).
- **Severe:** Catmint tea or pine needle decoction. Requires 2 doses over 24 hours.
- **Deadly:** Very rare herbs (swamp milkweed) OR Medicine check DC 20 (requires Herblore proficiency). Failure means patient still dies.

A wolf who recovers from a disease is immune to that specific disease for 1 season.

## 10. MATING, PREGNANCY & FAMILY 

### Courtship
- **Flirt or impress** (Charisma + Persuasion)
  - DC 12 (friendly)
  - DC 15 (neutral)
  - DC 18 (hostile or higher rank)
- Success: target is receptive.
- Failure: awkwardness or offense.
- Critical success: lasting attraction or immediate bond.
- Critical failure: hostility or humiliation.

### Mating & Conception
- **Mating act**: Fade to black. No roll required for the act itself.
- **Conception check** (optional, for randomness):  
  After mating, roll:
  - 1d20 + female's Survival/Constitution modifier + male's Survival/Constitution modifier.
  - DC 15 to conceive.
  - Advantage if both are in peak health (no exhaustion, well-fed).
  - Natural 1: complications (false pregnancy, illness).
  - Natural 20: multiple pups or unusually healthy litter.

- **Fertility timing**: Females can only conceive during spring (or a season defined by the GM).

### Pregnancy & Birth
- **Gestation**: 63 days (in-game time can be abstracted).
- **Pregnancy penalties** (optional): After halfway point, disadvantage on Dexterity and Strength checks for hunting/fighting.
- **Birth check** (Survival/Constitution, DC 12):
  - Success: normal delivery, healthy litter (1d4+1 pups).
  - Failure: difficult birth — mother gains 1 exhaustion level, litter size reduced by 1 (minimum 1).
  - Critical failure: mother or pups at risk — requires Medicine check (DC 18) to save them.
- **Raspberry leaves** (see Herb Compendium) grant advantage on the birth check.

### Pup Stats Inheritance
When pups are born, determine their base attributes (before any role assignment or aging bonuses) as follows:

**For each of the six attributes separately, use both parents' scores:**

**Method 1 (detailed):**
1. Roll 1d4.
2. If result 1–2 → Take the **lower** of the two parents' attribute scores, then add 0–2 (GM discretion or roll 1d3-1).
3. If result 3–4 → Take the **higher** of the two parents' attribute scores, then subtract 0–2 (GM discretion or roll 1d3-1).

**Method 2 (simpler, recommended):**
- Pup's base score = (average of both parents' scores, round down) + 1d4 - 2 (range -1 to +2).
- Minimum 1, maximum 10.
- Example: Father Strength 7, Mother Strength 5 → average 6. Roll 1d4 → 3. 6 + (3-2)=7 → Pup Strength 7.

**Pup starting totals:**
- Newborn pups have a total attribute sum of 6–10 (as per Pup role in character creation).
- Apply inheritance first, then adjust to fit the range by adding or subtracting small amounts (GM's discretion).

### Pup Development Stages

**0–2 months (Pup)**
- Role: Pup (attribute sum 6–10)
- Cannot hunt or fight
- Requires mother's milk or substitute (honey, goat milk)
- Bonding rolls available (see below)

**2–6 months (Juvenile)**
- Attribute sum increases by 1d4 points (distribute freely, max 10 per stat)
- Can learn skills (see Teaching below)
- May accompany pack but cannot participate in combat

**6+ months (Young Adult)**
- Choose a permanent role from the list (except Alpha unless challenged)
- Full attribute spread (must fit chosen role's total range)
- Can learn one additional skill proficiency of choice

### Parental & Pup-Raising Rolls
- **Bonding** (Charisma + Persuasion, DC 10): Success = strong parent-pup bond; failure = pup is skittish or distant.
- **Teaching a pup a skill** (Intelligence or Wisdom): Over a week of training, roll against DC 12. Success: pup gains a +1 bonus on that skill until adulthood.

### Rival Challenge (during mating season)
When two wolves compete for mating access to a receptive female, resolve with one of the following:

**Physical challenge** (opposed Strength + Hunting)
- Winner pins the loser.
- Loser must retreat or submit.
- If the female has a preference, she may give a +2 advantage to her chosen challenger.

- Winner forces the loser to flee via howling intimidation.
- Loser cannot approach the female for the rest of the day.

## 12. PACK REPUTATION & RELATIONSHIPS

Wolves are social animals. Track the following for your pack:

### Pack Unity (0–10)
- Starts at 5.
- **Increase:** Successful group hunt (+1), resolving internal conflict peacefully (+1), defending a packmate (+1), birth of pups (+1).
- **Decrease:** Selfish action that harms the pack (−1), losing a fight to a rival pack (−1), death of a packmate (−2), Alpha challenged (−1).
- **Effects:**
  - Unity ≤ 2: Disadvantage on group checks (howling, hunting pack).
  - Unity ≥ 8: Advantage on group howls and hunting pack rolls.
  - Unity 0: Pack may dissolve or wolves may leave.

### Rival Pack Standing (0–10 for each neighbor pack)
- Starts at 5 (neutral).
- **Increase (better relations):** Sharing territory without conflict (+1), helping against a common enemy (+2), diplomatic howl (Diplomat roll success, +1).
- **Decrease (worse relations):** Fighting over prey (−1), scent-marking over their border (−2), killing a rival pack member (−3).
- **Effects:**
  - Standing ≥ 8: Rival pack may share hunting grounds or help in fights.
  - Standing ≤ 3: Rival pack attacks on sight.
  - Standing 0: War — constant skirmishes.

## 14. OPPOSED & GROUP ROLLS

### Opposed Rolls
Both sides roll 1d20 + relevant modifier (+ skill bonus). Higher total wins; ties = stalemate.

- Natural 20: automatic win unless opponent also rolls 20 (reroll or tie).  
- Natural 1: automatic loss, plus something embarrassing/harmful.

Common scenarios:
- **Stealth vs. Perception** — Scout rolls Dexterity + Stealth; Guard rolls Wisdom (or Tracking).
- **Grapple vs. Escape** — Hunter rolls Strength + Hunting; prey rolls Dexterity + Survival/Constitution.
- **Intimidation vs. Wisdom** — Alpha rolls Charisma + Intimidation; rival rolls Wisdom (to resist).
- **Lie vs. Insight** — Liar rolls Charisma; listener rolls Wisdom.
- **Chase** — Both roll Dexterity; first to 3 wins catches or escapes.
- **Howling Contest** — Both roll Charisma; winner's howl dominates.
- **Scent over-marking** — Opposed Survival/Constitution.

### Group Checks
All wolves in a group roll the same check. If at least half succeed, the group succeeds.  
*Example: 4 hunters vs. DC 15 — two succeed, two fail → success.*

### Assisted Checks
A helper rolls DC 10 using the same attribute/skill. Success grants the primary roller advantage (roll twice, take higher). Failure has no penalty unless helper rolls natural 1.

### Pack Howl
Every wolf rolls 1d20 + Charisma. The highest single roll sets the howl's range. Add +1 to that roll for every 3 wolves in the pack. A natural 20 doubles the range.

### Hunting Pack (Coordinated Attack)
The lead hunter rolls 1d20 + Strength or Dexterity + Hunting. Each additional actively helping hunter adds +1 (max +5). If lead succeeds, prey is caught. If fail, each helper may roll a secondary DC 12 to wound prey for a future chase.

### Group Failure
If a group check fails, the GM may single out the lowest roller (or natural 1) for an individual consequence — injury, exhaustion, or predator attention.

### When Not to Use Group Checks
- **Individual stealth** — one noisy wolf ruins it; roll individually unless moving as a tight group.
- **Social challenges** — the Alpha (or speaker) rolls alone.
- **Healing a single patient** — only the Medic rolls; others may Assist.

## 16. WEATHER HAZARDS

Each hazard is an opposed roll: the wolf rolls the listed attribute, the environment rolls 1d20 + its severity bonus.

**Blizzard**
- Wolf rolls Survival/Constitution vs. environment +5 (severe) or +8 (whiteout).
- Success: find shelter or keep moving.
- Failure: +1 exhaustion, lose 1d4 hours.
- Critical failure: lost — separated from pack (Tracking DC 15 to reunite).

**Flood / rapid river**
- Wolf rolls Strength (+ Swimming) vs. environment +4 (swift) or +8 (raging).
- Success: cross safely.
- Failure: swept downstream, 1d4 damage, end up 1d6 miles away.
- Critical failure: near-drowning — must be rescued (opposed Strength) or lose consciousness.

**Wildfire smoke**
- Wolf rolls Survival/Constitution vs. environment +6.
- Success: no effect.
- Failure: disadvantage on Perception until clean air.
- Critical failure: collapse — dragged out, +1 exhaustion.

**Freezing rain / ice**
- Wolf rolls Dexterity vs. environment +4.
- Success: move carefully, no fall.
- Failure: fall, 1d4 damage, speed halved for 1 minute.
- Critical failure: sprained leg (see Injury Table).

**Extreme heat**
- Wolf rolls Survival/Constitution vs. environment +5.
- Success: endure.
- Failure: heat exhaustion — disadvantage on physical rolls until shade and water.
- Critical failure: heatstroke — unconscious 1d4 hours, needs water.


[6/14/2026 8:46 AM] lizardpelt
**Thick fog**
- Wolf rolls Survival/Constitution or Intelligence vs. environment +4.
- Success: stay on trail.
- Failure: lose 1d4 hours, may wander into hazard.
- Critical failure: separated from pack (Tracking DC 12).

**Thunderstorm**
- Wolf rolls Charisma vs. environment +6.
- Success: stay steady.
- Failure: frightened — must flee to cover, no actions for 1 minute.
- Critical failure: bolt blindly into hazard.

**Avalanche**
- Wolf rolls Dexterity + Survival/Constitution vs. environment +8.
- Success: escape.
- Failure: 2d6 damage, buried (Strength DC 15 to dig out; +1 exhaustion per minute buried).
- Critical failure: swept off cliff or into crevasse — death unless rescued within 1 round.

**Deep snow / whiteout**
- Wolf rolls Strength vs. environment +3 (knee-deep) or +7 (chest-deep).
- Success: normal movement (narratively slower).
- Failure: +1 exhaustion, speed halved for the day.
- Critical failure: torn claw or sprained leg.

**Quicksand / mud bog**
- Wolf rolls Strength vs. environment +6.
- Success: scramble out.
- Failure: sink deeper — needs rescue (opposed Strength).
- Critical failure: sink below surface — drowning risk, rescue within 3 rounds or die.

### Simplified Fixed-DC Option
If the GM prefers not to roll for the environment, use a fixed DC instead:

- Routine weather (light rain, mild wind) — DC 8 (no roll for healthy wolves)
- Challenging weather (blizzard, heatwave) — DC 15
- Desperate weather (whiteout, flood, avalanche) — DC 20
- Legendary weather (once-in-a-decade storm) — DC 25

The wolf rolls 1d20 + Survival/Constitution (or relevant attribute) against the listed DC.

# 17. SEASONAL EFFECTS SUMMARY

Quick reference for GMs to adjust gameplay by season.

### Spring
- **Mating season** – Rival challenges common (see Section 11).
- **Herbs** – Plentiful. +2 bonus to Foraging rolls.
- **Hazards** – Flood risk from melting snow. River crossing DC +2.

### Summer
- **Heat** – Extreme heat waves possible. Heat exhaustion checks (see Weather Hazards).
- **Prey** – Abundant. Hunting DC reduced by 2 for small prey.
- **Fire** – Wildfire risk. Random encounter chance of wildfire smoke.

### Autumn
- **Prey** – Fat and slow. Hunting DC reduced by 1 for all prey.
- **Herbs** – Begin to wilt. After first frost, Foraging DC increases by 2.
- **Caching** – Wolves may store food. Each successful hunt yields +1 extra day of food if cached.

### Winter
- **Foraging** – DC +5 (snow covers herbs). Only evergreens and bark available.
- **Prey** – Scarce. Hunting DC increased by 2. Some prey migrates.
- **Blizzards** – Common. Travel hazard checks occur twice as often.
- **Food requirement** – Wolves need 1.5x normal food to avoid exhaustion (e.g., 3 days of food for 2 days).
