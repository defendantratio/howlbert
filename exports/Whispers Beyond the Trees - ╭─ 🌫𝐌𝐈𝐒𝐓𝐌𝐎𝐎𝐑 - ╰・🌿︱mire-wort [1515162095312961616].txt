Appearance
Mirewort is lean and sinewy, built for crawling through cypress knees and sucking mud. His fur is a patchy olive-brown, permanently stained greenish-black from the swamp’s tannins, with weeping bald spots where shelf-fungus has taken root. His eyes are a pale, watery amber, always slightly unfocused, as if he’s listening to something just beneath the ground. The left half of his muzzle is scarred smooth: not from battle, but from a rot-lung treatment gone wrong. He walks with a slight limp, favoring his right foreleg, which ends in a paw missing two toes (lost to a snapping turtle in his youth). When he stands still, marsh grasses seem to grow toward him.


[6/12/2026 9:25 PM] lizardpelt
**Personality**
Patient, Morbid, Curious, Detached, Fervent

Mirewort speaks in a low, wet murmur, as if his words are bubbling up through peat. He never rushes, even in a crisis, he will pause to examine a wound from three different angles before acting. This infuriates the young Bog-Born but has saved more lives than any frantic rush ever could. He has a dark, gallows-humor that makes others uncomfortable; he laughs quietly while lancing abscesses or setting broken bones, and he names each plant he harvests after a wolf he failed to save.

His detachment is not cruelty but necessity. Mirewort has watched so many die, from rot-lung, from the Belly-Rip’s whispers, from the Maw’s own hunger, that he no longer flinches. He will hold a dying wolf’s head in his lap and tell them stories of the swamp until their eyes go glassy, then get up and chew moss for the next patient. He does not mourn openly. He mourns by remembering: every corpse becomes a herb name, every death a lesson.

Beneath the stillness, he is feverishly devout. He believes the Maw’s rot is not a curse but a gift: hat sickness is the land trying to speak, and healing is simply translating. He often mutters prayers while mixing poultices: “Maw, let this one listen. Maw, let this rot teach instead of take.”


[6/12/2026 9:28 PM] lizardpelt
**Backstory**

Mirewort was born to a low-ranking mother (a “Bog-Born” in local terms) who died of milk-fever when he was three weeks old. He was raised by a Drown-Sick (a wolf who had fallen into the Belly-Rip and emerged changed), a female named Hollowgaze. Hollowgaze could not hunt, could barely speak, but she could *find* things: roots that stopped bleeding, moss that pulled fever, mud that sealed wounds. She taught Mirewort everything by showing him, not telling: pressing a chewed leaf to his own cut pad, leading him to a particular rotting log at dawn, gesturing with her milky eyes at the fungus that glowed in the dark.


[6/12/2026 9:30 PM] lizardpelt
His Wound came when he was two. A litter of pups in the pack’s nursery den contracted the shaking-sickness, a seizure-illness that comes from drinking water too close to the Belly-Rip. Mirewort tried every herb Hollowgaze had shown him. None worked. One by one, the pups died, their bodies twitching into stillness. The last pup, a dark-furred female named Sedgepup, seized so violently that she bit through her own tongue. Mirewort held her as she bled out. That night, he went to the Belly-Rip’s edge and *listened*: not to jump, but to ask. The chewing sound grew louder, and then, for just a moment, he *understood*: the Maw does not hate. The Maw is simply *digesting*. The pups were not lost. They were *absorbed*.

He returned to the nursery den and gathered the dead pups’ bodies. He buried each one under a different herb plant, speaking their names into the soil. The next spring, those plants grew three times larger than any others. Mirewort became the pack’s unofficial healer, and when the pack’s leader at the time (the Rotmother, the alpha of Mistmoor) saw what he could do, she gave him a name: Mirewort, after the bitter root that cures the slow-fever but tastes like grief.

- **Morbid Detachment (Charisma -2):** His calmness in the face of death unnerves healthy wolves. The pack's second-in-command (the "Whisper-Jaw," the Rotmother's enforcer) watches him closely. When the Belly-Rip's influence clouds his mind, memory and clarity checks are penalized.

**Maw-Belief**
Zealot (but a quiet one). Mirewort does not preach or demand offerings. He simply knows the Maw is real in the way he knows mud is wet. He believes every illness is the Maw’s question and every cure is the wolf’s answer. He does not fear death, he fears misunderstanding the Maw’s lesson. When a patient dies despite his best efforts, he does not grieve. He asks: “What did I fail to hear?”

**RP Sample**
The mud sucked at Mirewort’s paws as he knelt beside the young wolf. The wolf, a male barely a year old, name of Murkpaw, had stepped on a submerged piece of human iron. Rusted, jagged, deep. The wound wept yellow and smelled of the green-sickness.

“Hold still,” Mirewort murmured, more to himself than to the trembling pup. His pale amber eyes traced the red lines climbing up Murkpaw’s leg. Blood poisoning. Maw’s teeth, it’s fast.

He reached into his herb pouch, a hollowed gourd slung over his shoulder, and withdrew a wad of chewed marsh-mallow root. “Open your mouth.”

Murkpaw stared at him, terrified.

“Open, or the fever opens it for you. And I’ll have to cut out your tongue to stop you biting it.” Mirewort’s voice did not change tone. He was not threatening. He was explaining.

Murkpaw opened his mouth. Mirewort pressed the bitter root under his tongue. “Chew. It will hurt. Good. Pain means you’re still in the Maw’s body.”


[6/12/2026 9:40 PM] lizardpelt
While the young wolf chewed, his eyes watering, his body shaking, Mirewort turned to the wound. He spat on his own paw (saliva, clean enough) and wiped away the yellow crust. Then he leaned close and listened. Not with his ears. With his forehead pressed against the hot flesh.

Chewing. Faint. The Maw was interested.

“You stepped on iron,” Mirewort said. “Iron is human bone. The Maw does not like human bone. You must apologize.”

“H-how?” Murkpaw sobbed around the root.

“By living. The Maw hates waste.” Mirewort pulled a curved thorn from his pouch, sterilized in swamp-fire smoke, and a length of dried sinew. “Now. I am going to cut the rot out. You will scream. When you scream, scream loud. The Maw likes to hear you fight.”

He cut.

Murkpaw screamed.

Mirewort smiled, a thin, sad, knowing smile, and whispered to the wound as he sewed: “Hear him, Maw. He’s still chewing back.”
