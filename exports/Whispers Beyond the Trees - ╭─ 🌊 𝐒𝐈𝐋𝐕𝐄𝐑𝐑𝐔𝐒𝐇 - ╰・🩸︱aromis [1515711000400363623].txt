**Backstory:**

Growing up, Aromis never knew the meaning of love or intense feeling. His mother abandoned him early; his sire was stern, cold, and threw his own beliefs at the small pup. Aromis grew into an emotionless, unempathetic male. At one year of age, he left his sire to find his own individuality: though he did acquire some fighting knowledge and hunting skills from him, the only things he could “thank” his sire for.

Then he met a strange wolf. Aromis was still young, and the wolf was much larger and stronger. He could not quite make out its appearance or remember what it looked like. Before he could retreat, the large wolf grabbed him by the neck, sinking deep canines into his flesh. As he hung in the wolf’s jaws, he reminisced about the one moment he had felt happiness, a fleeting peace. Then darkness.

When he opened his eyes, he was still alive. Around him were wolves with white-grey patterns and coats: Silverrush. He remained quiet in a corner of the strange den, surrounded by strangers. Instead of judgment, he was met with smiles and a warm welcome. Something new. But that did not change his purpose. He had one goal: revenge on the wolf that marked him.

**RP Sample:**

*Flashback to when he first came to Silverrush.*

His paws sank into the sand with every step. He glanced left and right, trying to familiarize himself with this unknown territory. It smelled of salt water — no, just river. Something he would never get used to. Hungry, he made his way toward the den to look for leftover meat. To his surprise, there was nothing.

*Where is the meat? The smell of deer corpse? Nothing is here,* he thought.

A nearby wolf approached, noticing his confusion — or perhaps his hunger. A calm, soothing voice spoke at his side: “Are you hungry, my dear?”

It was an older female, her dark grey coat overtaken by visible grey and white. She did not have much time left in this world. Pity.

“If it is food you are looking for, you will find your luck in the river.” She grinned softly, eyes closed.

*The river?* Aromis’s eyes opened with shock, ears perking high. Of course — the very place that reeked of sinister smells, which provided the only source of food.
